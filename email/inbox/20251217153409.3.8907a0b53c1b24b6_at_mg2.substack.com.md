The writing is technically flawless. But it is also totally predictable. It lacks the rhythm. It lacks the human touch.
I believe readers are smart. 
We can tell when an algorithm wrote something. We can easily spot AI generated content.
If you want to connect to your audience… and have people to actually believe you? 
Then you have to write against the machine.

Your goal is simple: make everything you write sound like a human wrote it. Even if you use AI to help. To do that, we have to avoid the most typical signs of AI writing.
Do so by following these style guidelines: 
1. Ditch the em dash
This is the long dash: “—”. Never use it. Seriously.
I know AI loves to use them. It’s a dead giveaway. Just use a comma or a period instead. Easy.
2. Stop the fragment lists
You know the style. Short, choppy sentences. Like this:
“Long hours. No pay. Just ink-stained hands and ambition.”
“He didn’t wait for permission. He didn’t wait to be ‘ready’. He just started.”
It’s a common AI habit. Don’t do it. Instead, write a full sentence. It sounds better. It sounds like you.
“He worked long hours for no pay, driven only by ambition.”
“He didn’t wait to be ‘ready’, he just started.” 
3. No mini-question intros
I’m sure you’ve seen it. It’s a common trick using a short question to set up the next sentence.
“The catch? It took me a while to find the right workout.”
“Within a few years? He owned his own print shop.”
It feels lazy. It’s a little too cute.
Just write it out. Say what you mean.
“The catch is that it took me a while to find the right workout.”
or
“Within a few years, he owned his own print shop.” 
4. Keep the poetry in your diary
AI loves to get fancy. It uses words that sound like they belong in a bad poem.
“Just ink-stained hands and ambition.”
“That day, the sun didn’t rise—it leapt over the horizon.”
This kind of language is a huge red flag. It makes your writing look like it’s trying too hard. It draws attention to itself. It sounds fake.
Write clearly instead.
5. Don’t force the fun
We all want to sound friendly. But forced familiarity just falls flat.
“Oh—and helped draft the Declaration of Independence. No big deal.”
It’s like a stranger giving you a high-five. Awkward.
Unless your audience is super casual, avoid this. Skip the “Cue the dramatic music” stuff. Keep it professional.
6. Mix up your voice
AI is like a robot reading a script. The tone is always the same. Boring. Humans don’t write like that. We speed up. We slow down. We get a little excited. We get serious. Vary your rhythm. This makes your writing feel alive. 
EXCEPTION: If you’re writing a super serious report, though, you can keep it steady.
7. Kill the clichés
If a phrase sounds like it came from a bad office meeting, get rid of it.
“Think outside the box.”
“At the end of the day.”
“Game-changer.”
These are tired. They make your writing sound lazy. Find a fresh way to say it.
8. Ditch the templates
AI uses sentence templates all the time. They are easy to spot.
“Whether you’re [A], [B], or [C], this is for you.”
“It’s not just X. It’s Y.”
“If you’re [X], [Y], or even [Z], this applies to you.”
“It’s not just [X]; it’s [Y] as well.”
“Here’s the thing: [statement that reframes the topic].”
“In today’s fast-paced world, [general claim about the topic].”
“At its core, [subject] is about [concept or value].”
“Picture this: [brief scenario that sets the stage].”
“It’s no secret that [common knowledge statement].”
“When it comes to [topic], one thing is clear: [assertion].”
“Simply put, [succinct summary].”
“In other words, [paraphrase of the previous idea].”
“Let’s face it: [uncomfortable truth].”
“That means [implication or next step for the reader].”
These are like little neon signs that scream, “A robot wrote this!”. Write sentences that are natural and unpredictable. That’s how real people talk.
