<h2><strong>The maintenance of everything</strong></h2>
<p>“Maintenance is absolutely necessary <em>and</em> maintenance is optional,” says <em>Whole Earth Catalog</em> and Long Now Foundation founder <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9zYi5sb25nbm93Lm9yZy9TQl9ob21lcGFnZS9Ib21lLmh0bWwiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTZ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Stewart Brand</a></u></strong> in <em><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9wcmVzcy5zdHJpcGUuY29tL21haW50ZW5hbmNlLXBhcnQtb25lIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjE3fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Maintenance: Of Everything, Part One</a></u></em>. Optional because we can put it off in the moment, necessary because putting it off for too long can lead to disaster.</p>
<p>We learn, in the book, of boats whose maintainability resulted in very different outcomes for three sailors competing to first circumnavigate the globe. We discover how maintenance attitudes in militaries can sway entire wars. Good and poor maintenance can both have profound consequences. </p>
<p>Maintenance is virtuous. But it’s also rarely seen as heroic. If it were, maybe we wouldn’t be so bad at it. <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly90aGVtYWludGFpbmVycy5vcmcvd2h5LWRvLXBlb3BsZS1uZWdsZWN0LW1haW50ZW5hbmNlLyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoxOH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Various explanations exist</a></u> for why we deprioritize maintenance, ranging from cultural values (we prize new invention over care for the existing), psychology and economics (we discount what isn’t immediately gratifying), and social class (we associate many maintenance jobs with minimum-wage work done by marginalized workers).</p>
<p>For me, many of these attitudes are embodied in Pixar’s 2008 animated film about a solitary garbage robot, <em>Wall-E</em>. The cheerfulness with which Wall-E performs his Sisyphean task of collecting and compacting tiny robot-sized cubes of trash makes us care for him. But he doesn’t become a hero until he leaves behind his duties to follow his love interest across the galaxy. At the end, we learn his quest is part of a larger story of failed maintenance—of the earth and its natural systems.</p>
<p>“Nearly everything worth maintaining is nested,” writes Brand, “in something larger, even more worth maintaining.”</p>
<h2><strong>Admin nights</strong></h2>
<p>For over six years, journalist and author <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY2hyaXNjb2xpbi5jb20vIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjE5fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Chris Colin</a></u></strong> has been hosting in-person gatherings akin to my digital ones. At these <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cud3NqLmNvbS9saWZlc3R5bGUvcmVsYXRpb25zaGlwcy9ob3ctdG8tdHVybi10aGUtYnVyZWF1Y3JhdGljLWdyaW5kLW9mLWxpZmUtaW50by1hLXBhcnR5LTcyMDVmNjkwP3N0PU41Q0Z3Zlx1MDAyNnJlZmxpbms9ZGVza3RvcHdlYnNoYXJlX3Blcm1hbGluayIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyMH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">“Admin Nights,”</a></u> Colin and friends gather with their laptops to cancel streaming service subscriptions, file insurance pre-authorization forms, dispute erroneous credit card charges, and, more generally, try to pull themselves out of the morass of maddening tasks that swallow modern life. </p>
<p>In the process, they’ve grown more aware of the sources of that madness, like the rise of subscription models and the breakdown of unions, regulators, and community groups that once shielded us from consumer abuse. Will AI eliminate this administrative friction, or only worsen it? </p>
<p>Colin does not dismiss the possibility that AI could make a meaningful difference, but said the group has developed a jaundiced long view of new technology. “I came to San Francisco in 1998, and I’ve seen a lot of things come through that promised to be amazing and weren’t,” Colin said to me over the phone. “Or things that promised to be amazing and were, and then were discontinued or evolved or broke after a few years.”</p>
<p>Even if AI ends up being “amazing,” I also wonder if smoothing over the friction would obscure its underlying causes, like the convenience-laden hoverchairs in <em>Wall-E</em> that numb senses and distract the humans from their world’s ills. Maybe it’s through the act of collectively dealing with that friction that Colin and his Admin Night group start to understand—and perhaps change—the broader systems to which most of us belong.</p>
<h2><strong>The COBOL cowboys </strong></h2>
<p>Those broader systems are facing a maintenance crisis of their own. Consider COBOL, a programming language designed in 1959, that handles transactions in <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGNtYWcuY29tL2FydGljbGVzL2libXMtcGxhbi10by11cGRhdGUtY29ib2wtd2l0aC13YXRzb24iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjF9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">43 percent of all banking systems worldwide</a></u>. It runs primarily on IBM mainframe computers, and still underpins much of the infrastructure behind public benefits like unemployment and food assistance that millions depend on.</p>
<p>Institutions haven’t modernized their COBOL systems because the process is a pricey, years-long affair. Meanwhile, the number of people in the world who have the expertise to maintain these systems shrinks by the month. When the situation is dire, organizations often call on the <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9mb3J0dW5lLmNvbS8yMDIzLzEwLzA5L2dlbmVyYXRpdmUtYWktY29ib2wtY29kZS13YWxsLXN0cmVldC1pYm0tbWljcm9zb2Z0LyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyMn0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">COBOL Cowboys</a></u>, run by 82-year-old cofounder and CEO <strong>Bill Hinshaw</strong> and staffed with some 600 COBOL engineers, many of whom got their start in the 1960s and ’70s programming those original mainframes. Heroes, for sure, but maybe only because they come in when these systems break.</p>
<p>So when Anthropic <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9yZXNvdXJjZXMuYW50aHJvcGljLmNvbS9jb2RlLW1vZGVybml6YXRpb24tcGxheWJvb2siLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjN9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">announced</a></u> last month that Claude Code can now automate the most time- and cost-intensive aspects of the COBOL modernization process, IBM’s stock fell by 13 percent, its<a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cudG9tc2hhcmR3YXJlLmNvbS90ZWNoLWluZHVzdHJ5L2JpZy10ZWNoL2libS1zdG9jay10YWtlcy1hLTEzLXBlcmNlbnQtd2hpcGxhc2gtYWZ0ZXItYW50aHJvcGljLWFubm91bmNlcy1hbi1haS10b29sLWZvci13cml0aW5nLWNvYm9sLWNvZGUtc3RvY2staGFzLXdvcnN0LWRheS1zaW5jZS0yMDAwLWFuZC1pcy1kb3duLTI1LXBlcmNlbnQtbW9tLWFuZC1jb3VudGluZyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyNH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"> </a><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cudG9tc2hhcmR3YXJlLmNvbS90ZWNoLWluZHVzdHJ5L2JpZy10ZWNoL2libS1zdG9jay10YWtlcy1hLTEzLXBlcmNlbnQtd2hpcGxhc2gtYWZ0ZXItYW50aHJvcGljLWFubm91bmNlcy1hbi1haS10b29sLWZvci13cml0aW5nLWNvYm9sLWNvZGUtc3RvY2staGFzLXdvcnN0LWRheS1zaW5jZS0yMDAwLWFuZC1pcy1kb3duLTI1LXBlcmNlbnQtbW9tLWFuZC1jb3VudGluZyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyNX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">single largest drop</a></u> in 26 years.</p>
<p>Not great for IBM’s business, but potentially transformative for people stuck on the other side of these systems. <strong>Adam Selzer</strong>, co-founder and senior director of Detroit non-profit <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9jaXZpbGxhLm9yZy8iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjZ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Civilla</a></u>, which helps local and state governments redesign how they administer benefits to their constituents, says that legacy technology is a significant barrier to change within these institutions.  </p>
<p>“[It] could be one of the most significant developments in civic technology in this decade,” says Selzer of the Anthropic news. “Or could lead to it.”</p>
<h2><strong>What’s Claw got to do with it</strong></h2>
<p>I personally haven’t gotten as far as overhauling the economy’s financial infrastructure. I’m still trying to contact my bank about an overbilled rental car and make my “iCloud Storage Full” alert go away.</p>
<p>So at a recent Digital Mending Circle, on the promise of a more frictionless life, I set up <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9ndWlkZXMvY2xhdy1zY2hvb2wiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6Mjd9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">OpenClaw</a></u>. Or started to set up is more accurate. It took several hours beyond my allotted life maintenance session for me to get <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSSBIaXJlZCBhbiBBSSB0byBEbyBNeSBDaG9yZXMuIE5vdyBJIE1haW50YWluIHRoZSBBSS4iLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MDEwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9jb250ZXh0LXdpbmRvdy9ob3ctY2xhd3MtdG9vay1vdmVyLWV2ZXJ5IiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">my Claw, Pip</a></u>, working. Once Pip was up and running, I had it send my partner and me morning briefings of our day’s schedule and childcare duties, and prepare budget summaries that we could go over in our weekly household meeting—augmenting a system we already had in place to help us stay afloat. </p>
<p>But sometimes Pip would format the budget summary in a weird way or forget how to access our transactions. Sometimes the morning briefings wouldn’t come—a result of my Claude Code account hitting its monthly limit or Pip incorrectly updating a setting in its own configuration file. At one point, two weeks’ worth of Pip’s memories simply vanished. In each of these cases, I would have to then go in and figure out why Pip broke, and how to fix it.</p>
<p>Instead of Pip helping maintain my digital life, I was maintaining Pip.</p>
<p>But in the process, I’ve become intimately familiar with the OpenClaw configuration file. I’ve come to better understand the cascade of markdown instructions that Pip reads for context, the art of managing Pip’s short- and long-term memory, and the way it uses skills and tools to do its work.</p>
<p>I’ve also come to learn, true to Brand’s words and Colin’s Admin Night group, the wider context around Pip: the ecology of frontier models, AI agents, new and existing software, and the people trying to use and build these tools. And once I could see this more clearly, I could start to imagine these systems working and interacting in a different way.</p>
<p>Then again, I still have to look into that rental car bill.</p>
