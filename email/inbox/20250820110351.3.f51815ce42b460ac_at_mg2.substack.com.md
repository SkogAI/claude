Today, I want to talk about what I think is the best way to build a business on your own. This applies whether you're working online, offline, or a little bit of both (which is what I prefer).
So, what is it? I won't make you wait.
It's info-selling. Or, to put it another way: consulting.
Let me tell you why. I've noticed that the most successful businesses, the ones that make a ton of money, usually have a few things in common. I've boiled it down to 11 key ingredients.
A great business model should have:
High profit margins (think 80% or more)
Recurring income, like a subscription
A process you can repeat for consistent results
Low costs to create your product
A large pool of potential customers
An easy way to deliver what you sell
The ability to grow through word-of-mouth
Ways to automate finding customers and delivering your service
A method to get customers for free
A "value ladder" with offers at different price points
A high-profit offer on the back end
So, why does consulting (or coaching, or being a "guru") fit this perfectly?
Because you can easily check all 11 of these boxes.
Think about someone like Tony Robbins. He's built an empire around this model. He uses a common strategy to get customers in the door for free. You've probably seen it before—lots of big names use it.
Once you're in his world, he has a whole range of products to offer. He starts with video courses. Why video courses? Because information feels valuable. Courses promise a result, a transformation from where you are now to where you want to be. We all want results, and we're willing to pay for them.
Plus, a video course is an amazing asset. You create it once, and it can keep making you money for years. It's almost passive income.
But it doesn't stop there. The model also includes:
A medium-priced subscription to cover your business costs.
A high-priced, exclusive subscription for a smaller group of clients. That's where the real money is made.
With this setup, finding customers can be automated. Delivering your product is too, since it's just a video course. The costs are super low, but the value feels high to the customer.
Now, I'm not a huge fan of how Tony Robbins built his business entirely around himself. If he's not on stage, the company doesn't run. That means he has to work like crazy all the time. He could have built a model that was less dependent on him... but hey, who am I to judge? It clearly works for him.
Could there be a better business model out there? I really don't think so.
Sure, we could talk about giants like Amazon, but that's a different league. This consulting model is something anyone can start. You can apply it to almost any niche and build a business that makes serious money.
It's the best solo business model because:
You can get customers for free, which also builds your brand's authority.
Your production costs are next to nothing.
You can have a low cost to acquire a customer but a high lifetime value.
You can build a real community of fans around what you do.
The profit margins are incredible.
You can run it from anywhere in the world.
