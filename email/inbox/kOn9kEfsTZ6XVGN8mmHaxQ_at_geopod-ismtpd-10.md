Hi Skogix,

I started building Boot.dev ( https://www.boot.dev?utm_campaign=website&utm_medium=email&utm_source=sendgrid.com ) a couple of years ago while my wife Breanna was working as an x-ray technician. She saw that I was having more fun, earning more money, and had more time off at my programming job than she did at the hospital.

She decided to learn to code, and I happened to be working as a hiring manager for developers. What luck! I knew exactly what she needed to learn to do well in the industry.

When I was looking at all the self-study options I could find, I wanted:

* Somewhere she could *write code and build projects* , not to sit in front of hours of tutorial videos
* Somewhere she could *have* *a ton of fun,* not languish while waiting for feedback and grades
* Somewhere she could *go deep on* *computer science fundamentals* , not be rushed through a 12-week bootcamp
* Somewhere she could *follow* *a complete learning path* , instead of wandering aimlessly without knowing what she didn't know

*I never found this place*. So I started building it, and called it Boot.dev.

My wife was Boot.dev's first student, she loves the courses, and I hope you do too.
