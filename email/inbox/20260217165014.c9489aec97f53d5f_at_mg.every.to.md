<h2>Voice as a keyboard</h2>
<p>You already know what you want to say. Monologue gets it out of your head and onto the screen inside the apps you already use—iMessage, Gmail, Slack, Notes.</p>
<p>Unlike Apple Dictation, which gives you a transcript, Monologue translates your speech into writing. The app cleans up repeated words, removes vocal tics like “um,” and adds punctuation. So you’re sending something clean, not jumbled. </p>
<h2>Context-aware formatting</h2>
<p>Monologue adapts to the way you write across different apps and contexts, so you do minimal editing. </p>
<ol>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>iMessage:</strong> Monologue creates writing that is short, casual, and conversational. You don’t have to explicitly indicate which punctuation you want, like an exclamation point or a period. It’s added naturally. </li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Email:</strong> Monologue translates your speech into structured paragraphs and clear sentences. Your tone sounds human. </li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Notes:</strong> Monologue turns your messy spoken thoughts into something you can actually use later, adding lists, headings, and clean bullets.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Your vocabulary</h2>
<p>We all have our own vocabulary—names of friends, acronyms we use, products we love, regional slang. </p>
<p>Monologue learns your words and keeps them with you. If you are using Monologue on Mac, the app syncs your lexicon across both.</p>
<h2>Whisper mode</h2>
<p>There are times you want to dictate without feeling like you’re performing, like when you’re sitting in a coffee shop or an office where you’d rather not broadcast the content of a draft email to your boss. Speak softly. Monologue’s whisper mode carries the big stick. </p>
<h2>Notes for long-form</h2>
<p>For that moment when inspiration strikes, but you’re not near a computer, Monologue lets you record longer audio like a voice memo. Use it for journaling or to capture your thoughts on a long walk, then scan the summary later.</p>
<p>Today, it’s simple on purpose. In the future, Notes will connect to third-party apps, becoming a part of the fabric of your workflows.</p>
<h2>The shortest distance between speech and writing</h2>
<p>The keyboard’s monopoly is ending. Voice notes are now a widely used and accepted form of communication. But there’s a problem on the receiving end. Your friends don’t want to listen to your near-podcast-length updates. The answer is to keep the voice input and make the text output better. </p>
<p>The most common gateway is coding—over 40 percent of Monologue usage in the past three months has been in the terminal or in tools like Cursor and Claude Code, where voice is the fastest way to tell agents what you need. From there, people graduate to emails and messages, then get comfortable enough with dictation to use it for everyday writing. I expect that progression to accelerate now that Monologue travels with you. </p>
<p>Like each product at Every, it’s built with taste—the same care for how something feels, not just whether it works—and quietly raises the quality of your life.</p>
