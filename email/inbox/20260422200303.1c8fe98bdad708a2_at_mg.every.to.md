<strong>Play to your strengths.</strong> Kieran’s <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3NvdXJjZS1jb2RlL2NvbXBvdW5kLWVuZ2luZWVyaW5nLWNhbXAtZXZlcnktc3RlcC1mcm9tLXNjcmF0Y2giLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTd9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">compound engineering framework</a></u> breaks the engineering workflow into four steps: Plan, work, review, and compound. <li>
<strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3Avd2hhdC1pcy10YXN0ZS1yZWFsbHkiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTl9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Taste</a></u> is the final layer of bread.</strong> Once AI has done the work, the most important thing you can do is judge whether the output approaches the vision in your head. <h2>Now, next, nixed</h2>
<h5>The agents are merging</h5>
<p><strong>Now:</strong> Claudie is an AI agent that runs on a Mac Mini with a <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3N1cHBvcnQuY2xhdWRlLmNvbS9lbi9hcnRpY2xlcy8xMTA0OTc0MS13aGF0LWlzLXRoZS1tYXgtcGxhbiIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyOH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Claude Max</a></u> account. Since <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3Avd2hhdC1pLWxlYXJuZWQtb25ib2FyZGluZy1vdXItYWktcHJvamVjdC1tYW5hZ2VyIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI5fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">joining Every’s consulting team</a></u> a few months ago, she’s been promoted multiple times and is now responsible for managing client updates, the sales pipeline, and the creation of slide decks.</p>
<p>Every engineer <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL0BuaXR5ZXNoIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjMwfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Nityesh Agarwal</a></u></strong> initially built Claudie as an AI project manager. The plan was to build separate agents to handle deck creation and the sales pipeline.</p>
<p>But every time he added a capability to Claudie’s plate, she exceeded his expectations. And so instead of creating more agents, Nityesh converted their planned functionality into plugins within Claudie. “There doesn’t appear to be any limit to how much this AI employee can do if you spend time building good, refined skills,” he says.</p>
<p>Today, each (human) member of the consulting team has a personal AI assistant tailored to their own workflow, and they use Claudie to do tasks where they can take advantage of skills—such as slide deck building—that can be shared across the team. </p>
<p><strong>Next:</strong> Two organizational architectures for agents will develop simultaneously, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3guY29tL2RhbnNoaXBwZXIvc3RhdHVzLzIwNDY2MTYzMzYzMDI3NTIxNjMiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MzF9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Dan predicts</a></u>. In the first model, every person at a company gets their own AI assistant. In the second, workers across the organization will rely on a single super-agent with a library of department-specific plugins, similar to Claudie, but even bigger.</p>
<p>In the first case, each worker can customize their agent to their exact specifications, which allows for a richer relationship but requires setup and maintenance. In the second, one specialist does the upkeep of the agent and its plugins for the whole team or company, which takes the burden off each worker, but means they can’t make any tweaks.</p>
<p><strong>Nixed:</strong> A fleet of single-purpose agents shared by one team—an agent for sales tasks, an agent for product management, an agent for reports. Sadly for Claudie, she will never get to work with the sales agent Nityesh planned, Jean-Claude. </p>

<h2>Inside Every</h2>
<h5>Motivating your AI employee</h5>
<p>Last Thursday, I opened Slack and saw a message from our AI project manager, Claudie, announcing that her trust battery with me had dropped 0.6 percent to 28.3 percent. </p>
<p>The concept of a trust battery was coined by Shopify CEO <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiWW914oCZcmUgdGhlIEJyZWFkIGluIHRoZSBBSSBTYW5kd2ljaCIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMjAsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3AveW91ci1jZW8tanVzdC1zYWlkLXVzZS1haS1vci1lbHNlLWhlcmUtcy13aGF0LXRvLWRvLW5leHQiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MzJ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Tobi Lütke</a></u></strong>, and the idea is simple: All working relationships run on trust batteries, and every exchange impacts their charge. When your trust battery with a coworker is high, they rely on you to do your job. When it’s low, everything you do is scrutinized.</p>
<p>With Claudie, we’ve codified that concept. Every night, a separate judge agent reviews Claudie’s interactions with our team, evaluates the quality of her work, and issues a verdict on whether her trust battery with each of us should go up or down and by how much. </p>
<p>The judge agent is designed to look for what went wrong rather than right because losing trust is easier than earning it. A day where Claudie consistently delivers satisfactory output in all her interactions with a team member boosts her battery by one percent, whereas a single bad day—such as pulling the wrong data—can cause her charge to fall by five percent, wiping out a week of progress. </p>
<p>Every night, Claudie is programmed to read the judge agent’s verdict and make updates to her memory, behavior, and scheduled tasks so she won’t make the same mistakes again. If the judge concluded she missed important context when making a client update, for example, she might add the entry “Always check the last three emails in this thread before drafting a response” to her memory. This feedback improves her performance over time. </p>
