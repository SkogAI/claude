Here’s the truth: shopping malls are dead, and e-commerce as you know it is on the death row as well.
Young people today aren’t browsing your website with the intent to buy anymore—they're scrolling feeds, laughing at memes, then buying something in the middle of a cat video binge.
If your business isn’t embedded right in that flow, you’ll be practically invisible in the new landscape.
E-commerce as we’re used to think about it—is dying. We’re entering a new phase that I call (in lack of better terms) attention commerce, and it’s the next big revenue engine.
The sharpest brains in the world are staking their claim. 
You want in? 
Here are 8 massive business opportunities that you should act on before everyone else wakes up.

1) Shoppable Livestreams
Live video has evolved. Brands and influencers now use it for selling on Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok, turning views into instant sales.
Why It Matters: Livestream shopping is growing fast, with projections showing a 25% jump next year. It’s an engaging way to sell products, answer questions, and close the deal—all in real time.
Opportunity: Use live events to launch products or run promotions. Engage with your audience, answer their questions,and watch your sales spike.
2) Selling Authenticity
People trust other people more than they trust ads—shocking, right? Reviews, photos, and videos from users create trust that polished marketing can’t achieve–but more importantly, aim for a balanced mix;
According to ComScore [ https://substack.com/redirect/1e91fa41-acbe-43b1-ba72-09419ae929f7?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ], engagements rise by 28% when customers are presented a combination of professional content and user-generated content.
Why It Matters: User-generated content builds a community around your brand and makes your products relatable. It's the modern-day word-of-mouth.
Opportunity: Encourage customers to tag your brand and share their experiences. Use their content to show what your product looks like in real life, outside the studio lighting.
3) Influencer-Led Social Shops
Influencers have loyal followers who trust their recommendations (well, unless they’re victim of the influencing-influencer scheme [ https://substack.com/redirect/b51e745d-3e9d-4efa-bcbd-bf57cf070288?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]…) Platforms now allow them to set up curated shops, effectively turning their profiles into personalized storefronts.
Why It Matters: This taps into ready-made audiences, giving you access to customers who are primed to buy.
Opportunity: Collaborate with influencers who align with your brand. Let them offer your products directly to their followers, adding an extra layer of trust to the transaction.
4) Augmented Reality Try-Ons
One of the biggest hurdles in online shopping is the inability to try before you buy. Augmented reality is changing that by allowing customers to virtually try on products.
Why It Matters: AR enhances the shopping experience and can lower return rates. Customers feel more confident in their purchases.
Opportunity: If you sell visual products like clothing or accessories, investing in AR can set you apart and boost sales.
⚠️ Word of warning to business owners: As I’ve personally deep dived into this on a global scale recently, I can tell you that the business case on this type of feature sets is still quite poor. The tech is still emerging and therefore costly. Accept a conservative ROI, or wait a bit more for the tech solutions to mature.
5) Hyper-Personalized Recommendations
Artificial intelligence can analyze user behavior to offer product suggestions that hit the mark. It’s the online equivalent of a good salesperson who knows exactly what you want.
Why It Matters: Personalization increases engagement and conversions. When customers feel understood, they're more likely to buy.
Opportunity: Implement AI tools to provide tailored recommendations. Make each visitor feel like your store was designed just for them.
6) Commerce Subscriptions
Why keep chasing new customers when you can deepen relationships with the ones you have? Subscriptions offer exclusive content, discounts, and early access, turning occasional shoppers into dedicated fans. We’ve recently awarded this model to being “The Most Powerful Business Model in the World [ https://substack.com/redirect/49da4165-cdb7-498d-9361-916597c3e107?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]”.
Why It Matters: Subscriptions provide steady revenue and foster brand loyalty. It's a win-win.
Opportunity: Offer meaningful perks through subscriptions. Make sure the value is clear and compelling.
7) Product Checkout As In-App Purchase
In a world where attention spans are shorter than a TikTok video, a clunky checkout process can kill a sale. Social platforms are refining in-app purchases so customers can buy without leaving the app.
Why It Matters: A smooth checkout reduces abandoned carts and increases sales. Convenience is king.
Opportunity: Integrate your e-commerce system with social platforms. The fewer clicks between browsing and buying, the better.
8) Eco-Conscious Shopping
Sustainability matters to today’s shoppers. More people are spending their money with brands that demonstrate genuine care for the environment.
Why It Matters: Being eco-conscious differentiates your brand. Customers want to feel good about their purchases, and they'll often pay more to support sustainability.
Opportunity: Be open about your sustainability initiatives. Share your practices, certifications, and efforts with your customers. It builds loyalty and appeals to those who value purpose alongside products.
The Window of Opportunity Is Closing
And there you have it. Eight attention commerce playbooks that could set you apart. 
Ignore them, and you might still be fine. I mean, the same way Blockbuster was fine when Netflix started mailing DVDs. 
The opportunities are here, and if you don’t grab them, just remember: someone else will…
While you’re debating it, some 19-year-old with a ring light and an Instagram following just sold out of the product you’re still thinking about marketing next quarter. 
Tick-tock, tick-tock.
