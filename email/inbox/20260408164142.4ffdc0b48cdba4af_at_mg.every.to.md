<h3>Agent moves</h3>
<p><em>Tips and patterns weвҖҷve picked up from working with AI agents every day.</em></p>
<p>When youвҖҷre thinking about what tasks to hand over to your agent, start with the papercutsвҖ”small recurring annoyances that add up over a day. One of mine was formatting screenshots to EveryвҖҷs style standards for use in the newsletter and on social media, so I gave my <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiRXZlcnkgSXMgSGFsZiBBZ2VudCBOb3ciLCJwb3N0X2lkIjo0MTA0LCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9wbHVzLW9uZSIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjo2MH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Plus One</a></u>, Margot, our formatting rules and asked her to learn them. </p>
<p>The problem is, Margot kept talking about the task instead of learning how to do it. She restated the formatting rules back to me, asked clarifying questions, and thenвҖҰstopped. </p>
<p>So I defined вҖңdoneвҖқ concretelyвҖ”вҖқI want you to be able to format screenshots according to our specificationsвҖқвҖ”and told her to stop deliberating and start, and when things broke, made her explain the failures in my language so I could make decisions about what to do next. One coaching session later, Margot formats any screenshot to spec on command.</p>
<p><strong>The lesson:</strong> When your agent is stuck, itвҖҷs usually talking when it should be doing. Coach toward actionвҖ”define what done looks like, cut off the deliberation, and make it build.</p>
