🖼️ Multimodality in Langfuse



Increasing multimodal support is a core item in our roadmap [https://langfuse.com/roadmap]. We've delivered a first step this
month that allows rendering of images within Langfuse [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2024-08-21-openai-multi-modal-traces].
 
As this is a core priority, we would love to hear from more teams that use or combine other modalities such as images, videos,
audio, file attachments, and code artifacts in their applications - reach out to us by replying to this email.
 


0️⃣ Zero Latency for Prompt Management



Langfuse prompt management helps many teams collaboratively version control and release their prompts. We have made a big
improvement to our SDKs which now serve prompts with no latency impact after first use
[https://langfuse.com/changelog/2024-09-04-prompt-management-zero-latency]. The SDKs cache prompts locally and refresh the cache
in the background.
→ You can take advantage of this by upgrading to the latest version of the SDK.
 


🗣️ Commenting



By popular demand, you can now add comments on traces, prompts and sessions [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2024-08-20-comments]
to collect research findings and collaborate more effectively.
What's next? Poking 👉?
 


💅 More Product Updates

 
 * Performance: We have invested heavily into performance of the Langfuse app over the past weeks. If there’s ever any table,
   query or feature that you feel is not in ship-shape, please contact us [https://langfuse.com/support].

 * [self-hosting] Headless-Initialization: You can now automatically initialize essential Langfuse resources
   [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2024-09-04-headless-initialization-of-self-hosted-deployments] (organization, project, user,
   and API keys) on startup using environment variables. This makes it easier to integrate Langfuse into your
   infrastructure-as-code and automated deployment pipelines.

 * Persisted Filters: Langfuse now persists your filters on core tables during your session. Thank you for everyone that
   approached us about this!

 * Integration with Vercel AI SDK: Langfuse supports the latest OpenTelemetry-based instrumentation of the Vercel AI SDK. This has
   proven very popular with users and we are excited to be the first platform to natively support
   [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2024-08-02-vercel-ai-sdk-integration] it. Side note: we are fans of the SDK and its
   abstractions, check it out if you develop in JS/TS.
