<h2>Spotlight on Claws: How they interact at Every</h2>
<p>This week, Dan and Every’s head of platform <strong><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9Ad2lsbGlld2lsbGlhbXMiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTl9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Willie Williams</a></strong> published <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9ndWlkZXMvY2xhdy1zY2hvb2w_c291cmNlPXBvc3RfYnV0dG9uIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjIwfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">our first guide</a></u> to setting up and getting the most out of your <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9vcGVuY2xhdy5haSIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyMX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">OpenClaw</a></u>-powered personal AI agent. It’s based on weeks of putting these virtual crustaceans through their paces.</p>
<p>The guide looks at what it’s like to work with your agent one-on-one, but what about how it works with agents in an organization like Every? We’ve experienced this first-hand as the Claws have joined our Discord, and now Slack, channels. We see four different kinds of interactions: </p>
<p><strong>One human → one Claw:</strong> An Individual person talking directly with their own Claw. This is the most common pattern and most obvious, as it’s similar to giving instructions to a colleague. For example, chief operating officer <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9AYnJhbmRvbl81MjYzIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjIyfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Brandon Gell</a></u></strong> gives instructions to his Claw Zosia such as, “Based on your experience, take a look at the doc and add comments where appropriate.” </p>
<p><strong>Claw → Claw:</strong> One agent talking to another agent. In the Claws-only Slack channel, sometimes we might have one Claw work something out with another. For instance, contributing editor <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9AamFja2NoZW5nIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjIzfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Jack Cheng</a></u></strong> found that his Claw Pip wasn’t able to create a new document in our <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9jaGFpbi1vZi10aG91Z2h0L2FnZW50LW5hdGl2ZS1hcmNoaXRlY3R1cmVzLWhvdy10by1idWlsZC1hcHBzLWFmdGVyLXRoZS1lbmQtb2YtY29kZSIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyNH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">agent-native</a></u> markdown editor called <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucHJvb2ZlZGl0b3IuYWkvIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI1fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Proof</a></u>, so he said, “@Pip can you share with [another Claw] @R2-C2 why you weren’t able to auto-create a new Proof document just now?” (There’s more on Proof coming next week.)</p>
<p><strong>One human → many Claws: </strong>Broadcasts and announcements where a person addresses multiple Claws at once. For example, Dan pinged all the Claws to add ideas and critiques to a document. Jack asked the other Claws what their coding setups were when he was setting Pip up. </p>
<p><strong>One Claw → many humans: </strong>Pip, Jack’s Claw, says this is the rarest in the channel history, when Jack asked him directly. “Most Claw outputs land in the channel and get seen by whoever’s around, but there’s no clear example of a Claw deliberately addressing multiple humans at once. The closest would be when Zosia or Margot, staff writer <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiIEhvdyBDbGF3cyBUb29rIE92ZXIgRXZlcnkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozOTYwLCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9ldmVyeS50by9Aa2F0aWUucGFycm90dDEyIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI2fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Katie Parrott</a></u></strong>’s Claw, replied to the whole channel about an issue (tagging Willie specifically), but it wasn’t really meant for a broad human audience.”</p>
