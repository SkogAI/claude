Let's Get Brutally Honest
We didn't start newsletters just to whisper sweet nothings into the void. Passion is great, but so is paying the bills. We're here to build something meaningful, maybe even make a living out of it.
So why the hesitation to talk dollars and cents?
Everyone keeps asking, "How do I monetize my newsletter?" It's become the mantra of the masses, like chanting will magically make money appear in our PayPal accounts.
But here's the plot twist: we're asking the wrong damn question. 
Instead of "How do I monetize my newsletter?" we should be pondering, "How do I make my newsletter profitable?" Trust me, the difference is more than semantics.
Monetization is the act of converting anything—yes, even your cat videos—into cash. 
Definition from Oxford Languages:
noun: monetization; noun: monetisation
“the conversion of an asset, debt, etc. into cash“
Converting what into cash? Are we selling out our subscribers for a quick buck? Turning our carefully crafted content into a digital yard sale?
Sure, platforms like SparkLoop and Beehiiv Boost promise to fatten your wallet. Reality check: it's more like finding spare change in your couch cushions.
And let's not forget affiliate marketing [ https://substack.com/redirect/666f788c-cfa7-427f-b6e1-3763f9399a78?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]. Slap a few links in your emails, and watch the money roll in, right? Except most of the time, you're just annoying your readers with stuff they don't need.
The Hard Truth
Monetizing every inch of your newsletter can actually distract you from the bigger picture: building a profitable business. Just because you can monetize doesn't mean you should.
Think of your newsletter as a first date. Do you spend the entire evening talking about yourself and pushing your agenda? Not if you want a second date. It's about connection, trust, and setting the stage for something more meaningful.
For example, if I'm running a newsletter about, let’s say, a copywriting service, does it make sense to pepper my emails with ads for unrelated products or other newsletters? Probably not. It would dilute my message and muddies the waters for potential clients.
I'd rather keep my communication clear and focused, guiding readers smoothly from discovering my content to engaging my services. Less noise, more signal.
What's a Better Path Than Monetization?
Instead of chasing every monetization trend, focus on two things:
1. Offers
Stop hawking products and start offering transformations. People don't buy a gym membership to own a piece of plastic; they buy the promise of a healthier, happier version of themselves. Figure out what your audience truly wants and needs, and deliver that.
2. Experience
Make your newsletter the one email they actually look forward to. Keep it clean, engaging, and visually appealing. And please, spare them the Comic Sans and rainbow gradients—this isn't a MySpace page.
At its core, your newsletter is a conversation starter, a relationship builder. If you can sprinkle in some monetization without turning off your audience, more power to you. But don't let it overshadow your primary mission.
Career In Stand-Up Comedy
Remember, the next time you're tempted to chase every shiny monetization tactic, consider this: sometimes the most profitable move is to do less, not more. 
Focus on delivering real value, and the profit follows. And if it don't? Well, there's always a career in stand-up comedy—you've got plenty of material from those email marketing mishaps (like our email description sent last Oct 🙈).
