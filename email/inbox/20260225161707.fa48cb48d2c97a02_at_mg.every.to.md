Students gather by вЂњhouse,вЂќ AlphaвЂ™s Hogwarts-style groupings based on personality and the progress theyвЂ™ve made on their projects.</p>
<p>Then comes the academic block: two to three hours of learning, chunked into 27-minute intervals with five-minute breaks. During each interval, students work through a learning platform the school calls <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBJbnNpZGUgYW4gQUkgSGlnaCBTY2hvb2wsIFRocm91Z2ggdGhlIEV5ZXMgb2YgYSAxNy1ZZWFyLU9sZCBGb3VuZGVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6Mzk1MywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vd3d3LnRpbWViYWNrLmNvbS8iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6Mjh9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Timeback</a></u>, which aggregates videos, articles, and quizzesвЂ”some built by Alpha, others curated third-party resources. Each week, students meet with their guide to plan which subjects to prioritize in their learning blocks. Students then dedicate the rest of the school day to their projects.</p>
<p>Mathew emphasizes that <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBJbnNpZGUgYW4gQUkgSGlnaCBTY2hvb2wsIFRocm91Z2ggdGhlIEV5ZXMgb2YgYSAxNy1ZZWFyLU9sZCBGb3VuZGVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6Mzk1MywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZnV0dXJlb2ZlZHVjYXRpb24uc3Vic3RhY2suY29tL3Avd2h5LWNoYXRncHQtaGFzLW5vLXBsYWNlLWluLWFscGhhIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI5fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">thereвЂ™s no AI chatbot</a></u> tutoring the children. He says the school tested that way of learning and it didnвЂ™t work. When left unfettered, students used the chatbot to cheat, and when restricted, the bot was useless. Instead, AI runs in the background of Timeback, customizing the content that each student sees and tracking their learning gaps.</p>
<h3>How Alpha keeps students honest when AI is everywhere</h3>
<p>Dan raises an obvious question: What stops a student from having an AI agent click around the videos and quizzes, especially for courses they arenвЂ™t interested in? Mathew says the school has layers of monitoring in place. Guides can see studentsвЂ™ screens in real time and track how long someone spends on each lesson. ThereвЂ™s also a <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBJbnNpZGUgYW4gQUkgSGlnaCBTY2hvb2wsIFRocm91Z2ggdGhlIEV5ZXMgb2YgYSAxNy1ZZWFyLU9sZCBGb3VuZGVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6Mzk1MywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vYWxwaGEuc2Nob29sL2Jsb2cvaG93LWFpLWFuZC1nYW1pZmljYXRpb24tdHJhbnNmb3JtLWxlYXJuaW5nLWF0LWFscGhhLXNjaG9vbC8iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MzB9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">вЂњwaste meter,вЂќ</a></u> a computer vision tool that monitors student activity to identify behaviors like mindless scrolling or being distracted by friends, and gives them real-time feedback about how much time theyвЂ™re wasting. </p>
<p>вЂњThe big thing about Alpha is we want to measure everything to make sure that youвЂ™re actually getting the experience you deserve,вЂќ Mathew says. вЂњIвЂ™ll be honest, itвЂ™s 90 percent motivation, 10 percent [education technology].вЂќ That motivation comes in a few forms. For subjects a student lovesвЂ”Mathew breezes through AP Psychology because it connects to his startupвЂ”the content is its own reward. For subjects they dislike, Alpha gets creative, incentivizing students with money toward their projects or invitations to outings the school calls вЂњFOMOs,вЂќ like hot chocolate on a rooftop around Christmas time.</p>
<p>The deeper motivator, at least for Mathew and his friends, is the flexibility theyвЂ™re afforded in return. Mathew, for example, negotiated with his guides to finish his first semester early so he can fly to San Francisco to work on his project full-time, then return to complete the next semester without losing credits.</p>
<h3>The Gen Z perspective on college, AI, social media, and books</h3>

<h4>Is social media a boon or a bane?</h4>
<p>Mathew agrees that social media has вЂњrottedвЂќ the brains of the younger generation, pointing to familiar problems: fractured attention, constant overstimulation, and the compulsion to compare yourself to curated versions of other peopleвЂ™s lives.</p>
<p>But he also wants to give it some credit. <h4>Does Gen Z still read?</h4>
