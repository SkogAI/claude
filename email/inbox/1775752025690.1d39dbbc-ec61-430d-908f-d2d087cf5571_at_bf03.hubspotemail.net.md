WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

Workflows that run themselves

EVERY MORNING

Standup brief

Agent searches Jira for issues updated in the last 24 hours, groups by shipped/in progress/blocked, writes a 5-bullet summary. Waiting before you sit down.

EVERY FRIDAY

Release notes from merged PRs

Agent reads PRs merged to main, categorizes by feature/fix/improvement, writes user-facing release notes in Google Docs. Half a day becomes 10 minutes of review.

BEFORE EVERY MEETING

Meeting prep on autopilot

Checks Google Calendar for your upcoming meeting, pulls the latest updates from related Jira and Linear tickets, reads the Notion spec, and writes a meeting prep doc. 45 minutes of tab-switching, gone.

EVERY WEEK

Stakeholder update email

Compiles completed Jira issues, drafts a weekly email in Gmail, saves as draft. The update that gets dropped first when you're busy now writes itself.

BEFORE EVERY SPRINT RETRO

Retro data, not retro vibes

Pulls completed and rolled-over issues, calculates completion rate by team member, identifies re-estimated items, writes findings to Notion. Data, not memory.

EVERY DAY

Customer voice to roadmap ticket

Agent queries HubSpot for support tickets and product feedback from the last 24 hours, groups by recurring theme, creates Linear tickets for anything mentioned by 3+ contacts with quoted examples, and logs trends to a Notion tracker. Product decisions based on real signal, not whoever's loudest in Slack.
