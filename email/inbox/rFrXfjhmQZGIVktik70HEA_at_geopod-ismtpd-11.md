Hello,

You cannot be fully productive while your teammates use different SSH clients. Why? The information required for your work is scattered. Fragmentation leads to outdated information, duplicated efforts, and, inevitably, frustration. It is called data silos, and it is the reason why *professionals spend 30% of their time looking for information*.

Multiple vaults in Termius allow you to *centralize data* — hosts, port forwarding rules, snippets, and credentials—into one place. It ensures the whole team has the latest info, making collaboration easier and reducing mistakes.

➕ Create as many vaults as you need for *flexible access control* to your information. For instance, let everyone work on the dev environment but keep production hosts limited to a few people. Help your teammates access only relevant information.

🔑 *Share credentials securely.* Credentials are not shared by default. Teammates must use credentials from their personal vault to connect to shared hosts. But if needed, you can share credentials within a vault for specific hosts, groups, and port forwardings.

By using vaults in Termius, your team can overcome the common pitfalls of SSH session management, ensuring a more efficient, secure, and collaborative working environment.
