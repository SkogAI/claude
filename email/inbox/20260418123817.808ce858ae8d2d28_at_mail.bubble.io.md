A $4B IPO and the lessons former Allbirds CEO Joey Zwillinger is carrying into 
his next company



 <https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoNVJrdvQQYnspOK4NkUSkzZkRYfrVQle>



Episode 8 of The New Build podcast is live a little earlier than planned — and 
you’ll see why! 🎤



Allbirds has been all over the headlines this month, first for selling off its 
footwear business and then for announcing a full pivot to AI infrastructure. 
And as luck would have it, we sat down with Joey Zwillinger, co-founder and 
former CEO of Allbirds, just before the news broke.

Listen now  <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6kujHK4qTpU>

Joey co-founded Allbirds in 2015, took it public at a roughly $4 billion 
valuation in 2021, navigated a multi-year turnaround through a volatile 
post-COVID market, and stepped down in March 2024.



He's now running Biologica <https://biologica.com/>, a women's hormonal health 
company he launched with his wife Liz at the end of 2025. And before any of 
that, he was an engineer working in biotech, which shapes how he approaches 
every decision as a founder.



Joey is refreshingly candid about what worked at Allbirds, what didn't, and 
the decisions he'd make differently now. We talk about hiring the right people 
early for the roles that matter most, why honest feedback gets harder to find 
the second time around, and the investor advice from 2015 he says he didn't 
fully internalize until years later.



If you're building something right now, his thinking on capital discipline is 
worth tuning in for.
