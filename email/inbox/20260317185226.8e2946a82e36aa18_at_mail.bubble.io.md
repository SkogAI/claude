Meet Sam Rudy. He spent over a decade booking recording sessions for some of 
the biggest names in music — and most of that time watching the same painful 
process repeat itself.



Finding a studio meant firing off emails to ten places at once, waiting on 
each of them to reply with their basic availability and rates, and managing 
dozens of threads. Eventually, that became reason enough to build the fix.



And that fix is ProStudioTime <https://www.prostudiotime.com/>, a marketplace 
for professional recording studios that gives artists and producers a way to 
find the right space without the back-and-forth.



Sam built it with his co-founder Ricky using Bubble — despite having no 
development background and initially assuming a platform like this would cost 
half a million dollars to build (spoiler: it didn’t).



In this episode, Sam talks through what the building process actually looked 
like, how he convinced more than 130 studios to join a platform still in closed 
beta, and why he thinks most founders wait far too long before putting 
something in front of real people.
