Most entrepreneurs chase the wrong dragon. They obsess over product features, slick branding, or the perfect sales funnel, convinced that's where the real leverage lies. They're wrong. 
The brutal truth is, your 'brilliant' innovation is likely dead on arrival if you haven't first understood the invisible force that dictates all success.

Today I want to talk about something big. It's a mistake I see most people make, and it absolutely kills their offers. For business owners, it's often the thing that keeps them stuck, preventing them from turning clients into converting [ https://substack.com/redirect/053ef433-866d-4461-a7c2-4677e2fbf936?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] offers.
Here's the deal: at its core, this whole game we play is super simple. It's just about finding problems and solving them. That's it. Nothing more, nothing less.
Find the problem. Solve the problem.
That's what we call a market. A market isn't just buyers and sellers. It's people with problems, and people with solutions. See how beautiful that is?
Now, if you've got a great [ https://substack.com/redirect/b8c10c23-6871-41c2-9671-585a92be72ff?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] niche market [ https://substack.com/redirect/b8c10c23-6871-41c2-9671-585a92be72ff?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ], you can have a pretty terrible offer and still make money. But if you have a phenomenal offer in a terrible market? Tough luck.
Think of it this way: you can have the best house in the city, but if it's in the wrong neighborhood, you won't get much for it. So, get the market right, and everything else becomes a million times easier.
And here's the kicker: the bigger the problem, and the better your solution, the more money you stand to make.
Problems come in two main flavors:
How many people have the problem? (Think widespread appeal)
How deep is the problem? (Meaning, how much does it mess with other parts of their lives?)
Both of these require some serious research [ https://substack.com/redirect/ed61b668-bc41-432a-a28f-94656fb2e530?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].
Solutions, on the other hand, have three dimensions:
How much time does it take?
How much energy does it take?
How much money does it take?
The ultimate problem is one that everyone has. And the ultimate solution? You just do one thing, and boom, it's solved.
And just like that... the whole world is talking about Ozempic.
Nordisk, the company behind it, is raking in $17 billion a year. That's a ton of cash. This combo of a massive problem with an ideal solution is so powerful, it's shaking up diet offers, personal trainers, and everything in between.
Why? Because, let's be real... why count calories, hit the gym, work out, and spend so much time and energy, when you can just take a shot every week and have it nuke your appetite?
Sure, there are downsides to taking it. But you get the point.
I've been mentoring a lot of people and working with companies doing hundreds of millions a year. And I've noticed a pattern. I've seen the one thing that separates those who reach the top from those who work for them.
Most business owners I know can create phenomenal offers with perfect sales funnels [ https://substack.com/redirect/999767b1-8db1-44ee-9d84-a3310db4d4c9?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]. But here's the catch: they can only do it once someone else finds the real problem and has the solution. Then, they step in to connect the two, and it works.
The real challenge for them? Finding the problem, finding the solution, and then connecting them.
So, many of you reading this have the chops to connect things. But not so much the skill of finding them.
And finding the real problem in the marketplace? That's like finding gold.
We could dive into market sophistication models, evolution, maturity cycles, and all that fun stuff. But when it comes down to it, it's pretty simple. Here it is:
To find the market problem [ https://substack.com/redirect/8d292f45-7bf8-45ef-91af-29b51dea03f9?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ], look for one thing and one thing only:
What is everyone trying to do but not getting the outcome?
That's it.
Check this out: Everyone wants to lose weight, but they can't lose weight.
That's the problem: they want to lose weight, but they can't.
Now, the mechanism of the problem is key. We need to know how to define a problem. Simply put, a problem is something that gets in the way of getting an outcome.
In weight loss, it's things like food cravings, having no energy to work out or count calories, and no time to exercise.
So, what causes all these things? Hormones, age, a broken metabolism, you name it.
If you look at every single weight loss product, the solution focuses on this.
And what do you know? Ozempic magically removes that problem and helps people lose weight.
Let's look at a few billion-dollar companies:
Everyone wants a vacation house, but can't afford it = AirBNB
Everyone wants information but doesn't have time to read it = ChatGPT
Everyone wants a new luxury car but doesn't want high payments = Car leases
See it? What does everyone want but can't get?
It's pretty simple: find the problem, solve it, and then use a high-converting landing page [ https://substack.com/redirect/8f48aee9-52f0-4951-81e2-14b72b22f7d4?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] to tell and show people how you solved it.
Done and done.
And just like that, you've got yourself a million-dollar offer.
Use this way of thinking to find gold in the market, and you'll find more gold than you know what to do with.
