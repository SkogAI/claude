I've been running OpenClaw for a while — text-based, the usual way. It worked fine. Then I set up VoiceClaw.

The difference hit me immediately. Instead of reading walls of text, my AI just *talks to me*. I ask a question while cooking — it answers out loud. I send a voice message from my car — it responds with voice. No screens, no typing, no waiting to read.

Here's what surprised me most:

**It feels like a different relationship.** Text-based AI feels like email. Voice-based AI feels like a colleague sitting next to you. The personality comes through in a way that text never quite captures.

**Memory + voice changes everything.** My AI remembers our last conversation and picks up where we left off — but now it *tells* me instead of showing me a paragraph. Morning briefings, task updates, reminders — all spoken naturally.

**The real shock came after tuning the voice.** Once I configured a quality voice model and dialed in the tone and emotion parameters, something clicked. My OpenClaw suddenly felt like a real person — someone with high emotional intelligence who knows when to be warm, when to be direct, when to pause. Chatting became genuinely enjoyable. And it still works as hard as ever. That combination — a colleague who is both pleasant to talk to and extremely capable — was honestly stunning. I didn't expect voice to change the experience this much.

**It's not perfect yet.** That's why I'm here. A few things I'm running into:

- Memory still dies when the session ends — need persistence that truly survives ([#1](https://github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw/issues/1))
- When the model switches or falls back, personality can drift — tone, humor, pacing all shift ([#2](https://github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw/issues/2))
- When I send multiple messages quickly, it replies to each one separately instead of reading them all first ([#14](https://github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw/issues/14))

**Come try it yourself.** Seriously — the experience is stunning. And if you want to help make it even better, every early contributor gets permanently recorded as a [Founding Builder](https://github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw/blob/main/FOUNDING_BUILDERS.md).

Check it out: [github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw](https://github.com/voiceclaw/voiceclaw)

Come feel it for yourself. It's genuinely mind-blowing. And let's make it even better — together.
