*API Bridge*

If you have a dynamic infrastructure where new machines spin up and shut down frequently, you know how time-consuming it is to manually update hosts in your vault. Now, you can automate host, group, and credential creation with an API Bridge. It helps keep your vault up to date with the latest infrastructure changes.

We ship the API Bridge as a Docker container ( https://hub.docker.com/r/termius/api-bridge ). This container is hosted in your environment and provides a REST API. It accepts information about your infrastructure, encrypts all data locally, and pushes it to your Termius vaults, ensuring that your sensitive information never leaves your environment in plain text.

The API Bridge is available to everyone with a Team account (including trials). Ready to get started? Set up ( https://account.termius.com/bridges ) your API Bridge today, and if you need any help, feel free to schedule a quick meeting ( https://meetings.hubspot.com/dmitry-babinsky/bridge-api ).

*Your Team's Weekly Report*

At Termius, we aim to make team collaboration easy. Over a year ago, we introduced vaults to simplify data sharing and access management. Now, it's time to help you stay on top of the changes in these vaults.

You may have noticed your team’s vault activity is now summarized in weekly reports, delivered straight to your inbox. These emails contain the changes you and your team have made. The report aims to help you monitor what's happening in your vaults, identify who has contributed the most to keeping the infrastructure up to date, and spot any unusual activity.

Initially, reports were sent daily. We quickly learned this was too frequent. So, we switched them to weekly by default. You can always customize the frequency and timing in your account settings ( https://account.termius.com/ ).

*Autocomplete: Helium vs. Hydrogen*

Over the past few years, we have focused on creating a modern autocomplete that boosts engineers' productivity in the terminal. We've added many completion types, like history, snippets, and passwords, while constantly iterating on the interface. However, after multiple small design adjustments, the autocomplete still felt boring and did not provide the engaging appearance and UX we strive for.

We chose to build the new autocomplete from scratch to avoid interruptions of the existing autocomplete audience who loves and uses it daily. Therefore, we decided to release a separate version. Now Termius comes with two autocomplete options.

Hydrogen is the old autocomplete, stable, and includes all your favorite features: path, history, snippets, and password suggestions, but it has no new UX.

Helium is the new autocomplete, with a new look, cutting-edge user experience, and latency-free input, but with limited suggestions. So far, it supports only history and paths. We frequently release new versions of it.

If you'd like to share your thoughts on the autocomplete experience, join our Discord Community. ( https://discord.gg/KpZw78pRcF )
