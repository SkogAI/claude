Bundling strategies, vibe coding hot takes, and why speed beats perfection







Our latest episode of The New Build just dropped — and this one's a 
masterclass in building products that compete with the biggest players in tech.


 Fuzzy Khosrowshahi is the CTO of Notion, the co-creator of Google Sheets, and 
former head of product engineering at Slack. He's spent decades building 
productivity tools used by hundreds of millions of people.


In this conversation, Fuzzy and I talk about:




Why bundling wins. Microsoft Office, Google Workspace, and now Notion all 
followed the same playbook: combining multiple products into a suite creates 
defensive moats that single-product companies can't match. But Fuzzy thinks AI 
might change this calculus entirely.


What happens when startups get acquired. Drawing on his experience at both 
Slack and Google, Fuzzy explains why product velocity fundamentally changes 
inside larger companies — and what Slack could have done differently as an 
independent company.


His honest take on vibe coding. He's skeptical of the name, but not the trend. 
Fuzzy shares where current tools fall short and why good engineers will remain 
essential even as AI improves.


The fastest path to validation. His core advice: Get something working as fast 
as possible so you can actually use it yourself. Perfection is the enemy of 
learning.
