<h2><strong>The data with value </strong></h2>
<p>The data sources with the most value share two traits: They’re high quality, and they keep growing. Reddit gets new posts. Shutterstock gets new image uploads. Games generate data from new sessions that reflect millions of human decisions. Models need to keep learning, and they need to learn from material that showcases intelligence at work.</p>
<p>The demands of AI labs can also influence what data has value—they are the biggest buyers. Currently, that is any data related to software engineering and math. If you can build an AI that writes excellent code and reasons through complex problems, you can use it to help build the next, better AI. That recursive loop is why labs are pouring so much attention into software engineering and math. Ahead of upcoming IPOs, the labs have widened the scope of their interest to “economically valuable work” in industries such as healthcare, professional services, and defense. </p>
<h2><strong>What makes a model great</strong></h2>
<p>So far, no one—not the labs, not anyone on X—has a settled definition of what makes a model great and what data is needed to get us there. </p>
<p>So the field is wide open for individuals to have a significant say in what models should prioritize, therefore shaping the future of AI. Do you want AI to handle customer service, use a browser, or draw <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3NpbW9ud2lsbGlzb24ubmV0LzIwMjUvSnVuLzYvc2l4LW1vbnRocy1pbi1sbG1zLyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyM30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">a pelican riding a bicycle</a></u>? </p>
<p>On the flip side, the lack of consensus means that conventional wisdom and social pressure have so far been large influences in what capabilities and skills we look for in models. Ultimately, however, what makes a model great is that it’s good at what people care about and get value from doing, a measure that will change over time. It will also influence which data has value for training models. </p>
<p>Researchers at Carnegie Mellon University and Stanford University <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2FyeGl2Lm9yZy9wZGYvMjYwMy4wMTIwMyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyNH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">recently mapped</a></u> existing benchmarks against what people actually get paid to do, and the gaps are enormous. Programming and math are massively overrepresented. AI’s suitability and performance at most work—including most of what you or your organization does every day, from planning a business trip to crunching data—has never been measured at all. </p>
<p>But anything we can measure, we can improve. These discrepancies are also where the next wave of valuable data is hiding. The groups that figure out how to measure those areas first will set the bar for it for a while and gain serious soft power. </p>

<h2><strong>How do you get value out of your data? </strong></h2>
<p>If you’re running a company with proprietary data, you have two paths. First, you can license it to a lab. Second, use it yourself. This data could be call transcripts that include the context of your decisions, support tickets that reveal your internal processes, or documents that lay out how you make budgetary decisions. </p>
<p>More teams are doing both. <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3ZpYmUtY2hlY2svY3Vyc29yIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI2fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Cursor</a></u>, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3guY29tL3RvYmkvc3RhdHVzLzIwMTY4OTMyOTAzOTg4MzEwNzE_cz0yMCIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyN30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Shopify</a></u>, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2hlbHAucGludGVyZXN0LmNvbS9lbi9hcnRpY2xlL2FpLWF0LXBpbnRlcmVzdCM6fjp0ZXh0PVBpbnRlcmVzdCdzJTIwTmF2aWdhdG9yJTJEMSUyMCglRTIlODAlOUNOYXZpZ2F0b3IsdGhhdCUyMHNpbXVsYXRlJTIwcmVhbGlzdGljJTIwbXVsdGl0dXJuJTIwY29udmVyc2F0aW9ucy4iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6Mjh9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Pinterest</a></u>, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2NvZ25pdGlvbi5haS9ibG9nL3N3ZS0xLTYtcHJldmlldyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyOX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Cognition</a></u>, and others are already training their own models on open foundations. The math makes sense for businesses. These models are cheaper and often better at the specific job, intellectual property stays in-house, and every use generates more training data that can be captured to improve the model even further. This <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL2d1aWRlcy9jb21wb3VuZC1lbmdpbmVlcmluZyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjozMH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">flywheel</a></u> is a moat. </p>
<p>The tools for this kind of training get easier every month. Companies like <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5wcmltZWludGVsbGVjdC5haS9ibG9nL2xhYiIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjozMX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Prime Intellect</a></u>, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3Vuc2xvdGguYWkvIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjMyfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Unsloth</a></u>, and Thinking Machines (<u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGhlIE1hcmtldCBmb3IgTWFraW5nIEFJIEJldHRlciIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQxMDYsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3RoaW5raW5nbWFjaGluZXMuYWkvdGlua2VyLyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjozM30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Tinker</a></u>) are building entire businesses around helping teams that aren’t AI labs train models that feel like they came from one.</p>
<p>Where this lands isn’t settled. Scale might keep winning, but the likely answer is that two paths coexist. Most tasks will run on AI that’s good enough, while fields like national security, medicine, and materials science will pay top dollar for the best model on earth. The teams that understand what their data is worth, and what it could become, are positioned either way. </p>
<p>If you want to find out where you stand, start with a simple audit: What does your company generate every day that a model couldn’t find anywhere else? This could include call transcripts where experts explain their reasoning, edge cases that your support team has solved, and documents that explain why certain decisions were made. That inventory is the first draft of either a licensing conversation or a training run.</p>
<p>There’s more at stake than revenue, as well. The companies that win this market end up doing something unusual: They become custodians of what humans know and how we think. They decide what gets measured, what gets preserved, and what gets fed into systems that more people use every day to make real decisions. </p>
<p>With that position comes responsibility—the responsibility to make sure we are keeping AI pointed at what people really need, and making sure the breadth of human experience shows up in the data, including the parts hardest to capture. </p>
<p>Most of those decisions haven’t been made yet. The people paying attention now are the ones who will get to make them.</p>
