The digital world is drowning in “free.” Free content. Free tools. Free advice. Everywhere you look, someone’s telling you to give it all away and trust the money will follow.
But there’s brutal truth… Most people who play that game end up broke, exhausted, and invisible.
Because the “free model” isn’t freedom at all. It’s a trap that steals your most valuable currency: time. And once you understand the economics behind it, you’ll see why only a tiny few rise to the top while everyone else gets crushed under the weight of their own production.

The Lie Behind “Free”
Ever heard the saying, “Give away free stuff, and you’ll get rich”? It’s a common idea, right? This “free model” sounds great on paper. But here’s the thing: only a few actually get rich. Most people? They just end up trying really hard and failing. Let me tell you why.
Think about it. The “free” model doesn’t mean no cost. It just means the cost changes. Instead of paying with money, you’re paying with time. Nothing is truly free. The currency just shifts, and sometimes, we don’t even realize what we’re spending.
Here’s a little secret: most of us have more access to money than we do to time. You can borrow money. Inherit it. Earn it. There’s so much money out there, and they print more every day. Just look at how much a dollar bought 20 years ago compared to today. It’s a huge difference.
Now, think about time. How much was an hour worth 20 years ago? And today? The value of time has gone way up. Money’s value? It’s gone down. So, time is the real boss here. It decides how everyone in your industry uses your “free stuff.”
We all have just 24 hours in a day. And those hours are split between social media, entertainment, work, family... you name it. We have to pick and choose. And when we choose one thing, we’re automatically saying no to others. It’s a tough choice, but it’s how life works.
The Consumption Rule No One Talks About
This brings me to a big point: all money comes from consumption, not production. We don’t make money just by creating something. The money rolls in when someone else uses or buys what we made. If we create something, and nobody consumes it, that’s a loss. Why? Because it took our precious time to make it.
Look around. This is true everywhere. Apple doesn’t make money when they build iPhones. They make money when we use those iPhones. Ford? They don’t make money when they build cars. They make money when we drive them. Facebook? They don’t make money from developing their platform. They make money when we use it. This rule applies to every single business out there.
So, if money comes from consumption, what happens when everyone starts producing a lot more? It’s a question worth asking. Just think about YouTube. There are way more videos that never get watched than those that do. Instagram? More people with no followers than with tons. X? Same story. More accounts with zero engagement than with a huge audience. And businesses? Most struggle to find enough customers.
Many YouTubers, Instagrammers, and Twitter users are giving away content. A few strike gold. But most? They’re just helping others get rich. Every game has winners and losers. The key difference? Understanding that money comes from consumption, not just making stuff.
The Hidden Winners in the Free Model
For me, it’s all about competing on economics. I’ve seen how big networks play the game. They’ll offer you access to their audience for your content. Then, they use your content to grow their audience. And once they have that audience? They might just move on from you. It’s a power shift, and it’s happening now.
The only way to really win? You have to pay for access within the network. Think paid ads. And to run ads at a big scale, you need good offers. Good offers make money through smart economics. So, competing on economics is your best bet. It takes time out of the equation and lets you grow big.
Now, the “free model” has a certain appeal. It feels like you don’t have to worry about ads or taking risks. Most people don’t have a lot of money, but they do have plenty of time. So, they jump on the “free” bandwagon.
Remember the “Free & Shipping” book offers from ClickFunnels [ https://substack.com/redirect/99ba3e21-adf9-4f53-a63b-cb1a9f4f3c01?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]? Many jumped on that. Most of those efforts? They’re gone now. The same thing is about to happen again. What happens when everyone is producing and giving away stuff for free?
Does that change how people consume? Not really. It just puts more value on consumption. So, those who produce are at the mercy of those who consume. That’s why you see YouTubers doing crazier and crazier things just to get attention. Eventually, they’ll burn out.
Status: The Ultimate Currency
The networks? They don’t care. The only ones who truly win are those with status. People will consume their stuff, whether it’s free or paid. Everyone else watches, wondering, “Why did that used to work, but it doesn’t work for me anymore?”
The easiest way to get status? Teach simple models that focus on production. Why? Because time feels endless, so these models get adopted fast. This boosts your status. Meanwhile, everyone else is competing fiercely with the same old model.
I don’t teach processes. Why? Because every process eventually breaks. And here’s the process that breaks them all: when too many people use it, it falls apart. The breaking point? When production far outweighs the ability to consume. I’ve seen this happen with every model over the last 20 years. The gurus who created them? They’re gone too.
What AI Does to the Market
This same idea applies to AI. All these creators are saying AI will create wonders. And it will! But it will also mess up the production/consumption balance. Honestly, I don’t care much about AI itself. What I care about is what AI does to the market. How it changes things. The same rules apply.
Again, money comes from consumption. When production goes way up, the value of what’s produced goes down. So, many creative skills will lose their value. Why? Because consumption won’t keep up with the massive increase in production. People who used to charge $50,000? They might now be charging $25,000. Too much production, not enough people buying.
Only those with status can keep their prices high. These people will use their money to run paid ads. Everyone else will be scratching their heads, thinking, “What happened in the last three years? Why am I stuck?” Now you know. When production explodes, everyone’s value drops. But if you understand how things really work, you can easily get ahead.
The End Game
There’s a bigger game at play here. Ask yourself: What happens to the value of what you make when everyone else can make it too? What happens to your special advantages? And what happens to you?
The end game is clear: those who can buy audiences will be on top. They’ll be seen by everyone on the networks and can sell to everyone. The rest? They’ll be fighting for tiny bits of attention. Why? Because of all the “free” stuff flooding the market.
Look at YouTubers. Or even better, the music industry. Artists sometimes have to pay other artists just to be featured on their songs. When I say “buy audiences,” I don’t just mean ads. It also includes paying for podcast spots. Guest appearances? They’ll become paid gigs. Again, you need good economics to cover those costs.
So, the idea of just hopping on podcasts and “giving away free stuff”? That won’t work either. Everything is going to be monetized. If you’re going to play in a game where every part costs money, you need two things:
Learn to think. This helps you create, change, and fix processes and models.
Know how to compete on economics. When that day comes – and it is coming – you’ll be ready.
The game has always been simple: those who can pay more to get customers, get all the customers. That’s food for thought. You do the dishes.
