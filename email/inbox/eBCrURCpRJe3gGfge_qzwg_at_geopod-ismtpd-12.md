🤖 DevOps AI Agent Gloria
------------------------

At Termius, we've always been obsessed with engineer productivity. For years, we've helped millions manage their infrastructure efficiently. The recent revolution with AI coding agents transformed software development, giving developers superpowers to ship faster than ever, while DevOps has stayed behind. Users keep telling us the same thing: the workload keeps growing, the team remains the same, and the tooling is not keeping up.

We're excited to introduce Gloria – an experimental AI agent designed to handle simple infrastructure tasks so you can focus on complex problems.

What Gloria Does
----------------

Gloria lets you offload routine DevOps work. Tell Gloria to install Apache and configure it on a specific port on your web servers. It handles package installation, dependencies, service configuration, and verifies that everything works according to your request.

Gloria learns along the way, maintaining successful execution paths and building knowledge about your infrastructure to solve problems faster and more accurately over time.

How It Works
------------

Gloria runs in Termius Cloud, allowing you to SSH into it from any terminal. Start a deployment from your desktop and check progress later using Termius on your phone.

Unlike general-purpose AI tools, Gloria understands your infrastructure context: the operating systems, installed packages, and service configurations. We built it specifically for infrastructure management, keeping real DevOps workflows in mind.

Note: Gloria is not yet integrated with Termius Vaults, so you will need to add the infrastructure manually.

Join the Limited Preview
------------------------

We're launching Gloria in a limited preview to a small group of Termius users who are open to AI and want to delegate routine tasks. We want to stay in a tight feedback loop with you and keep refining the product based on your needs.

🎮 Multiplayer: Real-time collaboration in the terminal, reimagined
------------------------------------------------------------------

Troubleshooting is easier with a teammate — but when you’re remote, getting that help has always been messy. Screen sharing? Clunky. Copy-pasting commands from chat? Slow. Dictating commands over a call? Awkward.

That’s why we’re excited to introduce *Multiplayer* — our biggest leap yet in real-time terminal collaboration. It feels like sitting side by side, looking at the same screen, and passing a keyboard back and forth.

Why we reinvented collaboration
-------------------------------

A few years ago, we launched *Live Terminal* , our first attempt at bringing real-time collaboration to the terminal. It was inspired by shared documents, where everyone can type at once. However, the terminal doesn't work like a document — it operates through commands and responses. And that's what was wrong about Live Terminal:

* *Everyone could type at the same time* , creating chaos instead of clarity.
* *No visual clue* of who's in control.

So we went back to the drawing board.

Multiplayer: Collaboration that makes sense for the terminal
------------------------------------------------------------

The new Multiplayer is tailored for the terminal experience:

* *One person types at a time* — no more command collisions.
* *A visible cursor* clearly shows who’s in control.
* *Passing control* is as simple as handing over a keyboard.
* *No setup needed* — start a session and invite teammates instantly.

The future of remote troubleshooting
------------------------------------

With Multiplayer, debugging, teaching, or responding to incidents together finally feels as natural as sitting at the same desk.

Try it now ( https://termius.com/beta-program ) in the latest beta build and experience the smoothest terminal collaboration yet.
