<p>Hi Emil,</p>
<p>Have you noticed? The mymind notes editor has been quietly improving every day. Here are just a few of many recent updates:</p>
<p>✦ <strong>The new markdown shortcut menu</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/kOenTHYH53Vg6zwWvehogY;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>Type a / in any note view to bring up the new shortcut menu. Select an option (also works via keyboard arrows) and keep on typing. You'll find new formatting styles there too, including code blocks and inline quotes.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zakbcgw4DDA" style="color: #FF5924;">→ See how it works in this video </a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>✦ <strong>Support for images in notes</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/iNSlhADluA9bKeHQpsM6zm;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>This was #1 on your list of requests: You can finally drag &amp; drop an image into a note, or use the / new menu to add one. It even works in the Quick Note.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mDRQjNzfkfU" style="color: #FF5924;">→ Watch the video </a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>✦ <strong>Support for Markdown files</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/6qNqaP8DU04e1kuopgFSJP;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>You can now import markdown files into mymind. Just drag the file straight onto the screen to upload it. Supported markdown will convert into a new note card, with the file name as the card title.</p>
<p> <br />
✦ <strong>Notes are now analyzed and context enriched</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/8SN9PZCQfUlbpRF3BtxfUT;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>Your mind now enriches your notes with automatic tags and summaries that expand and deepen the context, making them easier to find later.</p>
<p>Now you can search for broader ideas or themes (not just exact keywords) and find relationships between your notes.</p>
<p>Of course, privacy was our priority here. The notes analyzer only uses Bedrock, which gives us the strictest guarantees. The content never leaves AWS, is never logged, and is never shared with the model providers. This was a serious upgrade and a foundation for more to come.</p>
<p>P.S. This is a Mastermind-only feature.</p>
<p> <br />
✦ <strong>Ghost drafts for new notes</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/4cdpF4DGlzkbaCeBfJKq5k;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>If you draft a new note and forget to save it, your mind now automatically saves it as a ghost draft in the background. This affects new Quick Notes as a double safety measure. Existing notes are always auto-saved as you edit them.</p>
<p> <br />
✦ <strong>Improved focus mode for longer drafts</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/iZRozNinG2zerxKfvVMjOk;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>We made lots of little tweaks to help you stay in the zone and seamlessly get your thoughts down in Focus Mode.</p>
<p>Our own focus is still on the notes editor, and we'll be shipping some cool new updates within the next week or two for you.</p>
<p>In the meantime, we'd love to hear your feedback on the latest updates. (We pass along every message you send to the team and it always makes them happy.) Just reply to any of our emails and a real person will answer you.</p>
