Cloud Endpoints are here!

Cloud endpoints are persistent and always-on, and you can manage them from either the dashboard or API. Because they run persistently on the ngrok network, they’re perfect for enforcing your most important traffic management rules—like AuthN/AuthZ or rate limiting—from one place before routing traffic to your upstream services.

Yes, we’ve been talking about these for a while, but now that they’re fully live, we all can’t wait to see how you use them.

A cornucopia of Kubernetes

Our K8s team shipped 0.17.0 of the ngrok Kubernetes Operator. It’s an absolute milestone in our work to support cloud-native and get your clusters using endpoints, which are much easier to spin up and configure than edges.

- New CloudEndpoint and AgentEndpoint CRs help you provision ngrok resources quickly and K8s-natively.
- The Operator now auto-converts all your Ingress, Gateway (API), and LoadBalancer definitions directly into endpoints so you can use all our newest features without rebuilding ingress from scratch.
- With deeper Gateway API support, like ReferenceGrant for cross-namespace references, ngrok slots nicely into—not overwrites—your existing configs.
- Bindings like public and internal now work identically on all CRs and K8s native implementations.
