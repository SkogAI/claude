Let’s cut through the noise: most content advice is garbage.
You’ve been told to post consistently, be authentic, and “provide value.” Cool. But here’s the kicker—no one’s telling you how to actually  [ https://substack.com/redirect/4d873c0e-9ed0-4b01-bdc3-92bbc5b71f5c?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]sell [ https://substack.com/redirect/4d873c0e-9ed0-4b01-bdc3-92bbc5b71f5c?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] without sounding like a used car salesman.
Here’s the truth: content creation is a balancing act between two forces—content that grows [ https://substack.com/redirect/8fb38c08-bde8-4d6a-ba36-eae19bebb510?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] your audience and content that gets them to open their wallets.
And guess what? Most creators are terrible at balancing both.
But not you. Not after this. Not with the exact formula attached as a spreadsheet at the bottom.
Let’s break it down.

Let’s get one thing straight: content creation is a messy, human, and sometimes hilarious process.
Imagine you’re a bartender. You’ve got two drinks to serve your crowd—one that keeps them hanging around and one that gets them to tip big.
Let’s call these growth content and sales content.
Most creators? They’re still figuring out the recipe. Too much of one, and the vibe’s off. Too little of the other, and the night falls flat.
Here’s how to mix it right.
What’s Growth Content?
You’re at a party. Someone yells, “Free shots!”
Everyone turns. That’s growth content.
It’s the flashy, attention-grabbing stuff that makes people stop scrolling and say, “Hey, this is cool.”
Examples:
Quick tips: “Here’s how to fix your Wi-Fi in 2 minutes.”
Viral trends: Jump on that TikTok dance. (Yes, even if you look ridiculous.)
Relatable memes: “When you realize it’s only Tuesday…”
The goal? Get eyes on you.
Growth content is like a firework—bright, loud, and gone in seconds [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb2aad85-909d-4e39-92f4-12207ca9b17a?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ]. But it leaves an impression.
What’s Sales Content?
Now, you’re not yelling about free shots. You’re sitting at the bar, telling a story about how you quit your 9-to-5 to start a business.
The person next to you leans in, nods, and says, “Tell me more.”
That’s sales content.
Examples:
Personal stories: “Here’s how I failed, cried, and then figured it out.”
Deep dives: “Let me explain why this strategy works (and why most people get it wrong).”
Opinions: “Here’s why I think [insert hot take].”
The goal? Build trust [ https://substack.com/redirect/1b6ecaec-1a27-4d2e-80a8-feb24e1b61e1?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].
Sales content is the slow burn. It’s the conversation that makes people think, This person gets me.
Why Opinions Are Your Secret Sauce
Here’s the thing: your take is yours alone. Nobody else has your story, your scars, or your weird sense of humor.
Say you’re a fitness coach. Sure, everyone’s talking about squats and protein shakes. But only you can share how you went from hating your reflection to owning it.
That’s the magic.
When you share your opinions, you’re not just another voice in the crowd. You’re the one people remember.
How to Balance Growth and Sales Content
You don’t have to choose. It’s not either/or. It’s and.
1. Plan Your Mix
Growth content: Flashy, fun, and fast.
Sales content: Honest, deep, and personal.
Example:
Monday: A meme about Mondays (growth).
Wednesday: A carousel on how you landed your first client (sales).
Friday: A personal story about a failure that taught you something (sales).
2. Keep It Clear
Growth content should be snackable. Sales content can be a full meal, but don’t overcomplicate it.
3. Be You
Write like you talk. If you curse, curse. If you’re sarcastic, lean into it. Perfection is boring.
Tips for Killer Growth Content
Think like your audience. What makes them stop, laugh, or click?
Tutorials: “Here’s how to fold a fitted sheet without losing your mind.”
Trends: Put your spin on that viral challenge.
Entertainment: Share a blooper reel or a behind-the-scenes fail.
Example: If you’re a baker, post a video of a cake collapsing. People love a good disaster.
Tips for Killer Sales Content
Sales content needs soul. It’s not about pushing a product; it’s about connecting.
Stories: Share the messy middle, not just the highlight reel.
Opinions: Don’t be afraid to ruffle a few feathers.
Education: Teach something only you know.
Example: If you’re a photographer, talk about the time you showed up to a wedding with the wrong lens. Then explain how you salvaged the day.
Why You Need Both
Think of it like this:
Growth content is the neon sign outside the bar. Sales content is the bartender who remembers your name and your drink.
If you only focus on growth, you’ll have a crowd but no one buying rounds. If you only focus on sales, you’ll be talking to an empty room.
You need both.
The Takeaway
Content creation [ https://substack.com/redirect/ce75a35b-5de5-412a-9875-d59914a8c8b0?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] isn’t a polished, perfect art. It’s raw, real, and sometimes chaotic.
Mix growth and sales content, and you’ll build an audience that sticks around—and buys what you’re selling.
Now, go make something that's authentically you.
(And maybe pour yourself a drink while you’re at it.)
