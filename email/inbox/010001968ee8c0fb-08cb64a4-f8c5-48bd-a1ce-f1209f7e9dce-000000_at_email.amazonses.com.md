<p>Hi Emil,</p>
<p>How do you come up with an idea out of thin air?</p>
<p>You don't. You're drawing from your repertoire.</p>
<p>Think of your repertoire as the accumulation of everything you've ever seen or experienced, distilled to its essence.</p>
<p>People with good ideas have a system of building their repertoire and using it to make unexpected connections – even if it's only happening in their head.</p>
<p>✦ For us, it's happening in mymind. ✦</p>
<p>Today, we have a little guide showing you how we build our personal repertoires – this is a longer one (appx. 4 minute read time) so put on some soothing music and settle in ◡̈</p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>Rule #1. Your repertoire should be private</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/dotVmWE2rb2eclSMSt0gHY;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>Yes, entirely private. If it's open to other people, you're immediately limiting yourself. You're trying to impress or meet expectations.</p>
<p>It's like decorating a hotel room: It may be pretty and comfortable, but it's curated for other people. It's not <em>you.</em></p>
<p>When your repertoire is private, there's no pressure to perform for anyone. You don't have to worry about being on trend, intellectual, consistent or attractive.</p>
<p>It's a beautiful feeling. Once you embrace it, you'll find all kinds of weird things in your repertoire. Real, interesting references that push you outside the agreeable box. You're now free.</p>
<p>(Besides: Nobody should be able to trace your steps when it comes to your work. You're the magician. Keep your secrets to yourself.)</p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>Rule #2. Don't force it</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/7xnNez9VIddecq2WQeeyVA;1000x_.png?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>You can't build your own repertoire in a few hours, days or even weeks. You can't use someone else's Pinterest board, or dump a thousand images from some aesthetic website in there.</p>
<p>This only clutters your repertoire and makes it meaningless.</p>
<p>The point is that everything in there means something to <em>you</em> specifically. That's where the magic happens. And you can't skip the magic.</p>
<p>Only once you've given a reference your &ldquo;stamp of approval&rdquo; does it land in your collection. Millions of living room references exist online, but the 100 or so you have in your repertoire are the best, according to your taste.</p>
<p>So how do you decide what goes inside?</p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>Rule #3. Collect ambivalently and passively. Not indifferently.</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/apUldNTxJY4eY9yRvc49Ym;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>Usually, we collect inspiration only for specific projects. But this means we're already narrowing our funnel considerably.</p>
<p>When building your repertoire, you want your funnel open at all times. Passively build it up every day. Eventually, you can then narrow <em>that</em> down for a specific project.</p>
<p>Every time you see an image or reference online that makes you feel something – save it. Ambivalently means you may have strong but opposing feelings toward something. Save those things. It just has to make you stop for a brief moment.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/8Cli3ppuJ9IeWJeIF3tRcP;1000x_.png?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>That image may have no immediate purpose. It's not for a current project. The only meaning is that it may mean something to you in the future. And this is the secret.</p>
<p>mymind works the same way your real mind works: It's a sponge of references, ideas, visuals, stories and things you care about. Everything you don't care about (feel indifferent about) isn't soaked up and slowly fades into oblivion.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>Rule #4. No rules</strong></p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/kfRsEfQUeime5xTdNK9mma;1000x_.png?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>Don't save what you think should <em>should</em> be saving. Don't over-analyze what you save, or what it means about you. Just save it. The significance will be revealed later.</p>
<p>There's no need for organization here either – that only disrupts your natural flow. If a particular reference has no immediate purpose, there's no folder or category for it. And you don't want organizing to add friction to your saving process.</p>
<p>In mymind, you just search to find it later. No organization needed.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>How to use your repertoire</strong></p>
<p><strong>✦ The random inspirational browse</strong> - The beauty of having your own personal repertoire is that you can browse through it whenever you feel uninspired and need a pick-me-up.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/7aRRNJPCFPBdeUUePsrch2;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>✦ Searching for specific references</strong> - Let's say you're working on a branding project and quickly need references for certain materials or packaging techniques. You just head to mymind, type in what you're looking for and immediately find it.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/6VBbcDJdx73evDogtwLyWP;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p>You never created a folder or collection titled &ldquo;brand inspiration,&rdquo; which would be too broad anyway. In this case, you're looking for a certain material, or pattern, or technique, so that's what you search for. And there it is, because mymind sorted it for you.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/j6DJpHfFynIdO4z87phwpd;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>✦ Serendipity -</strong> In <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kt1Ix86mrw" style="color: #FF5924;">Serendipity mode</a>, mymind resurfaces one random thing from your mind at a time. This introduces some chaos or chance into your inspiration process, which is always good for new ideas.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/itOxFEI9VlfcypaGV8jgSL;1000x_.png?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>✦ Same Vibe -</strong> The <a href="https://mymind.com/videos#same-vibe" style="color: #FF5924;">Same Vibe feature</a> takes one image you've saved to your mind and collects others with the same look &amp; feel. Here is where you might make connections or find the exact mood for a project you're working on. It was all there in your repertoire because you saved it – and mymind pieced it all together for you in an instant.</p>
<img src="https://carbon-media.accelerator.net/00000000001/4UjcSLRPhfdf0BLLRGIzGu;1000x_.jpeg?auto=webp" width="960" height="463" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;"/>

Then start passively but intentionally building your secret repertoire. Your future self will thank you for it.</p>
