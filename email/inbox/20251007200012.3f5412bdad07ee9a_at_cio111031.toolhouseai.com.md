Product Updates
***************

Introducing Code Editor

You’re now able to directly modify your agent files in Agent Studio! You can make fine-grained changes to your agent by editing its code directly. Our built-in checker will help you identify and even fix common errors.

Whether you're refining behaviors, tweaking capabilities, or debugging in real time, this update puts full control at your fingertips for faster iteration and smoother deployment.

Community showcase

Our new and improved landing page now features a dynamic gallery that showcases top agents from the community, giving you  examples of the most helpful creative public agents created by the community out there.

You can even click on each agent to clone it and build your own!

Agent Previews

You’re now able to talk to your agents before deploying them! Test conversations directly in Studio to see how your agent responds, refine its behavior, and make adjustments on the spot.

Validate prompts, flows, and integrations early so your agents are ready to perform once deployed.

Where were we this month?
*************************

HackMIT

This month the Toolhouse team proudly sponsored HackMIT, one of the largest collegiate hackathons. Over the course of the exciting 48 hour event, we supported a community of talented student hackers, helping them bring ambitious projects to life. It was inspiring to see so many innovative ideas and collaborations unfold, and we were thrilled to provide resources, guidance, and encouragement to help participants push their skills and creativity to the next level.

Ditto Hackathon

This month the Toolhouse team went to Exa HQ to support Build Club’s Copy That SaaS event. We partnered with Lovable to help Vibe coders build fully working web apps, all without writing a single line of code.

PDD Launch Party

This month the Toolhouse team helped host the launch party for Prompt Driven Development! Guests enjoyed live demonstrations, including PDD legacy to cloud migration powered by Kimi K2 on Groq hardware and Toolhouse Agent Studio turning YAML specifications into fully running agents. Attendees also received pizza, limited edition PDD x Toolhouse tees and stickers, and Groq credits. The event showed how PDD streamlines software development, reduces technical debt, and allows developers to work more efficiently with AI assisted workflows.

Dumb Hack

Toolhouse supported The Dumb Hackathon, a fun event where participants built absurd and creative projects. Hackers used Toolhouse’s Backend as a Service platform to quickly create and deploy AI powered agents, taking advantage of features like RAG, memory, and prompt optimization. The platform helped bring wild ideas to life while making the event both fun and technically impressive.
