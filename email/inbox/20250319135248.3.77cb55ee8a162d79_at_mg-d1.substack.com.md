Superstructures — the boring, obvious giants everyone knows — are cheat codes for lazy geniuses. 
I would even say; old marketing wisdom is overrated, just because of this.

Forget the fluff. This approach cuts out unnecessary steps and focuses on the fundamentals that convert.

Here’s a secret weapon most offers ignore (until it’s too late)
Let’s talk about “superstructures.” No, not skyscrapers or bridges. These are cultural, technological, or behavioral touchstones so deeply embedded in society that they come preloaded with trust and attention. 
Think Facebook, Gmail, TikTok, or Amazon—platforms billions already use daily. Or trends like keto, veganism, or meditation. You don’t need to explain what these are; you just need to plug into their momentum.
Most offers and digital products [ https://substack.com/redirect/09e3748d-a609-4613-b064-a133a749aa07?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] fail because they skip this step. They try to invent something entirely new instead of hitching a ride on what’s already rolling. But here’s the twist: You don’t have to pick just one superstructure. You can stack them.
Take Tim’s approach, an old client of mine. Faced with a product that didn’t neatly align with a single superstructure, he didn’t scrap his strategy. 
We layered multiple cultural pillars together. Instead of explaining his idea from scratch, we anchored it to familiar giants: Instagram, plus the trust in Gmail, plus the cultural wave of keto. 
Each superstructure lent its credibility, shrinking the gap between “What is this?” and “Why should I care?”
The lesson is to recognize that borrowing cultural gravity is super smart (and not cheating). Why build a new audience when you can meet yours where they already are?
The hard math of stacking superstructures (no magic required)
Let’s break it down with real numbers. My team and Tim’s team took two existing concepts, mashed them together, and watched the metrics spike. 
For example:
Copywriting + AI
Agency services + Facebook
Coaching + Instagram
That’s it. No reinvention, no complicated jargon. Just pairing familiar ideas. 
The results? A jump from a 10% opt-in rate to 40%. Conversion rates went from 3% to 12%. 
But the real shocker was the upsell performance: A product that once converted at 3% as a standalone offer suddenly hit 35% as an upsell. Even the second upsell climbed to 30%.
Why does this work? People already understand coaching, Instagram, agencies, and Facebook. Combining them doesn’t confuse audiences—it reduces friction. You’re not asking customers to learn a new language; you’re speaking theirs.
Why start from zero when you can start from yes?
Eliminating pre-requirements (your customers hate friction more than you do)
So, we faced this problem. A digital product offer (but could as well be an affiliate marketing [ https://substack.com/redirect/d1542fd8-646e-4d4e-9287-9dded39e9feb?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] och paid newsletter [ https://substack.com/redirect/a4877c00-3edd-4faa-803d-c89ce21992bf?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] offer) had a narrow audience because it required users to have two specific skills: hours to rewrite templates and knowledge of how to edit them. This shrank potential market and drove up costs per acquisition.
We had two fixes on the table:
Add more templates to the bundle. But this meant constant updates for new markets—a time suck.
Teach customers to rewrite the templates themselves. But that would ask users to invest even more time upfront, adding friction.
The solution? We plugged into a superstructure everyone was already talking about: AI. 
By integrating AI tools [ https://substack.com/redirect/73578bfd-b358-4738-91b7-97eb9a100a3e?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] that automatically adapt templates, we erased the need for users to manually rewrite copy or possess editing expertise. Suddenly, the offer wasn’t just for experts—it was for anyone.
No more workshops. No more tedious tutorials. Just a product that met customers where they were, using a tool (AI) they already recognized. The result? A wider market, simpler onboarding, and a offer that finally scaled without begging users to climb a learning curve.
Old offer:
Niche templates
Required hours of customization
Limited to specific industries
Low usability (“Why buy this if I still have to do all the work?”)
New offer:
AI prompts compatible with any business
Generated ready-to-use copy in the user’s voice
Zero editing skills required
High utility (“Copy, paste, done.”)
The clincher? Their original templates still had value. Users could now feed them into AI tools, making those templates 10x more useful overnight. No workshops, no tutorials — just a product that met customers where they already were: eager for results, allergic to busywork.
This update turned an offer burdened by lengthy pre-requisites into a solution that delivers value immediately.
