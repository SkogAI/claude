The secret to earning more as a freelancer
Choosing between short form and long form work is a smart way to actually earn more as a freelancer.
In this business, you usually have two groups of people. You have the people who own the products and the people who do the freelance work. I want to talk to the freelancers today. If you own a product business, you might still find this useful too.
I was emailing with one of you wonderful readers last week. He asked me how to get famous in this industry. He wanted to know how to find more clients or start charging a lot more money.
You obviously have to get better at your craft and learn how to think. Those are basic requirements. But the real answer comes down to the type of work you choose to do.
Why you need to collect trophies
A few years ago when I was freelancing, I spent a lot of time talking with other freelancers. I remember many younger guys (yeah mostly guys for some reason) asking me how to build status so they could finally make good money. They thought they had to spend all their time networking or showing off.
I told them the truth. You just need to get trophies. That is the only thing that matters.
A trophy is a win you can point to. If you want people to respect you, you need to show them real results.
Find a client who needs a specific goal met
Work hard to hit that goal
Use that success to prove you know your stuff
I remember when I first started out and felt like I was shouting into a void. I thought I needed a fancy brand. I quickly realized that a single great case study was worth more than a thousand social media posts.
Once you have those wins, you don’t have to chase people. They start looking for you. It makes the whole process of raising your rates much easier because the proof is right there.
The speed of short form work
Your success as a freelancer depends on how many trophies you have. If you can’t show a potential client what you’ve done, they won’t care who you are. They definitely won’t hire you.
It sounds simple, but there is a trick to getting these wins faster. It comes down to picking short form work over long form work.
Short form projects are quick. You can finish them in a week or two and move on. This means you could grab 2-4 trophies every single month. Long form projects are different. They might take three months to finish. If you only do those, you might only have four wins to show for an entire year of work.
Think about it like this. Would you rather have four happy clients talking about you every month or just four every year?
How to build a reputation fast
I saw this happen with a guy I knew. He focused on being a short form freelancer. He offered clients a specific type of funnel that was quick to build. He also wrote short, punchy sales bullets for their products.
Because he kept things simple, he could finish a whole project in about seven days. He didn’t reinvent the wheel with his ads either. He used the same basic templates and just swapped things out.
He worked fast
He got results quickly
He moved to the next client immediately
I once tried to take on a massive project that took nearly six months to finish. By the time I was done, I was exhausted. Even though it went well, I only had one thing to show for half a year of my life. If I had spent that time on smaller projects, I could have had a dozen testimonials by then.
Speed helps you build a reputation. When you can point to ten different people you helped in a single season, you look like an expert. It makes you look busy and in demand. 
And that is how you get the power to tell a new client that your price just went up.
Comparing the two paths
