<p>In 1893, a printer named <strong>Horace Hart</strong> pinned a piece of paper to the wall of the Oxford University Press room where books were printed. It told his compositors—the workers who assembled metal letters by hand into the trays that would be inked and pressed against paper—how to handle the decisions that came up hundreds of times a day. When to capitalize. Where to put a comma. How to abbreviate, hyphenate, and spell. </p>
<p>That piece of paper grew into a 200-page manual, which people called <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2VuLndpa2lwZWRpYS5vcmcvd2lraS9IYXJ0JTI3c19SdWxlcyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjo2fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Hart’s Rules</a></u>. It’s now one of the oldest continuously maintained style guides in print. </p>
<p>The term “style guide” itself didn’t <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2Jvb2tzLmdvb2dsZS5jb20vbmdyYW1zL2dyYXBoP2NvbnRlbnQ9JTIyc3R5bGUrZ3VpZGUlMjJcdTAwMjZ5ZWFyX3N0YXJ0PTE4MDBcdTAwMjZ5ZWFyX2VuZD0yMDE5XHUwMDI2c21vb3RoaW5nPTAiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6N30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">appear in print</a></u> until the 1930s. For most of publishing history, people said “style manual” or “style sheet.” But whatever you called it, the function was the same: Make the rules explicit so the output stays consistent, no matter who—or what—is producing it.</p>
<p>When we hear the word “style guide,” we tend to think about rulings on the Oxford comma or how to abbreviate “United States.” But Hart was solving a machine problem. Oxford was printing <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2VuLndpa2lwZWRpYS5vcmcvd2lraS9Ib3JhY2VfSGFydCIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjo4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">272 books a year</a></u>, and dozens of compositors worked the presses. His guide made sure output stayed consistent no matter who was at the press that day.</p>
<p>Every time a new machine changed how writing was produced and distributed, someone wrote a style guide to bridge the gap between what the writer intended and what the technology could do. When the telegraph <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5icml0YW5uaWNhLmNvbS90b3BpYy9Bc3NvY2lhdGVkLVByZXNzIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjl9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">arrived in newsrooms</a></u> and could cut off mid-transmission, reporters learned to front-load the essential information, producing the<a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5wb3ludGVyLm9yZy9yZXBvcnRpbmctZWRpdGluZy8yMDAzL2JpcnRoLW9mLXRoZS1pbnZlcnRlZC1weXJhbWlkLWEtY2hpbGQtb2YtdGVjaG5vbG9neS1jb21tZXJjZS1hbmQtaGlzdG9yeS8iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTB9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"> </a><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5wb3ludGVyLm9yZy9yZXBvcnRpbmctZWRpdGluZy8yMDAzL2JpcnRoLW9mLXRoZS1pbnZlcnRlZC1weXJhbWlkLWEtY2hpbGQtb2YtdGVjaG5vbG9neS1jb21tZXJjZS1hbmQtaGlzdG9yeS8iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTF9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">inverted pyramid</a></u>—the journalistic principle that the most urgent idea in a story should come first, followed by less pressing details. Eventually, we got the <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2VuLndpa2lwZWRpYS5vcmcvd2lraS9BUF9TdHlsZWJvb2siLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTJ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">AP Stylebook</a></u>. When screens <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5ubmdyb3VwLmNvbS9hcnRpY2xlcy9ob3ctdXNlcnMtcmVhZC1vbi10aGUtd2ViLyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoxM30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">changed how people read</a></u>, web style guides made everything shorter and scannable.</p>  <div class="ea-block normal">

<p>Language models have opened a new gap. They can generate writing, but they <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3AvdGhlLXNjaWVuY2Utb2Ytd2h5LWFpLXN0aWxsLWNhbi10LXdyaXRlLWxpa2UteW91IiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjE3fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">can’t generate </a></u><em><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3AvdGhlLXNjaWVuY2Utb2Ytd2h5LWFpLXN0aWxsLWNhbi10LXdyaXRlLWxpa2UteW91IiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjE4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">yours</a></u></em>. Left to their defaults, they converge on the same polished, generic, interchangeable prose—the kind that opens with “In today’s rapidly evolving landscape” and closes with a three-part summary no one asked for. And once again, the solution is a good style guide. Like Hart’s Rules or the AP Stylebook before it, an AI style guide takes what the writer knows and puts it in terms the machine can use. </p>
<h3>Surfacing the hidden patterns </h3>
<p>Previous style guides focused on issues that were solvable with conscious, mechanical decisions. Hart could tell his compositors to spell “colour” with a <em>u,</em> and the job was done. The AP could tell reporters to lead with the most important fact. </p>
<p>In contrast, the most distinctive markers of a writer’s style <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3AvdGhlLXNjaWVuY2Utb2Ytd2h5LWFpLXN0aWxsLWNhbi10LXdyaXRlLWxpa2UteW91IiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjE5fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">tend to be subconscious</a></u>: word choice, sentence rhythms, and patterns that fall into place while you’re focused on meaning. Researchers have found that humans are roughly twice as linguistically varied as machines, sentence to sentence. The gap narrows with better models, but it doesn’t close.</p>
<p>The only way to make a model approximate your unique voice is to surface those hidden patterns, name them, and write them down as instructions.</p>
<p>I stumbled into this when I started <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL2V2ZXJ5LnRvL3dvcmtpbmctb3ZlcnRpbWUvaS1mZWQtbXktZXNzYXlzLXRvLWNoYXRncHQtdW50aWwtaXQtbGVhcm5lZC1teS12b2ljZSIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyMH0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">feeding my essays to ChatGPT</a></u> and asking it what it noticed. Over weeks, it surfaced patterns I’d never consciously seen, such as how I structure an argument to progress from personal experience to broader stakes, or tend to favor long, winding sentence structures (a temptation I’ve calibrated my style guide to help correct). I copied what felt true into a Google document and continued the conversation, pushing back on some observations and adding in details I knew I wanted my writing to avoid. Eventually, I uploaded it all to a Claude Project, and the writing that came back started to sound like me. </p>
<p>It’s surprisingly hard to describe your own voice from a blank page. You know what your writing sounds like. You can recognize it instantly. You just can’t explain it in terms a model can use—and vague descriptors like “smart” and “conversational” don’t make the AI better at approximating you.</p>
<p>It’s all well and good to say, “You should make a style guide,” but we also know that when you’re trying to get somewhere, it helps to have a map. So we wrote a step-by-step guide to building your own AI style guide from scratch—what it is, what goes in one, how to build one by letting AI interview you, and how the practice can grow with you and your writing process. It draws on the systems we’ve built at Every and for <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IHRvIEJ1aWxkIGFuIEFJIFN0eWxlIEd1aWRlICIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjQwMTQsInBvc3RfdHlwZSI6InBvc3QiLCJ1cmwiOiJodHRwczovL3dyaXRld2l0aHNwaXJhbC5jb20iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjF9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Spiral</a></u></strong>, our AI writing tool. By the end, you’ll have a working document you can hand to your chatbot, agent, or OpenClaw assistant and test on your next draft.</p>
<p>Hart’s compositors didn’t need to understand why they spelled “colour” with a u. They followed the rule. But writing an AI style guide forces you to answer a harder question: <em>Why do I write the way I do?</em> The document you end up with may be addressed to a machine, but the person who learns the most from it is you.</p>
