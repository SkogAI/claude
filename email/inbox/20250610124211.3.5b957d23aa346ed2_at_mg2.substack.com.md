All Successful Solopreneurs Can Build Irresistible Offers
So look. Every successful solopreneur out there knows how to build one.
Nicolas Cole? Grew a multi-million dollar ghostwriting agency by cold messaging strangers.
His secret? Irresistible offers.
Dan Koe? Sold millions worth of digital products.
Same thing. Irresistible offers.
What makes an offer irresistible?
Simple. It:
Grabs attention fast
Keeps people reading
And makes them buy
No, attention spans aren’t the problem. Weak offers are.
What Is an Irresistible Offer?
It comes down to two things:
What you’re selling
How you describe it
Nail both? You win. 
Most people don’t even get one right. They push what they want to sell, and explain it like they’re defending a bad idea at a dinner party.
Good news for you?
Even if you just get one of these right, you’ll stand out.
But why stop there?
By the time we’re done, you’ll know how to hit both.
We’ve All Seen Irresistible Offers Online
You know the ones. They grab your attention [ https://substack.com/redirect/69941b11-22e1-47b1-9f4e-bb4053c6278f?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ], spark a little curiosity, and suddenly you’re reading every word.
Here are a few examples of short, irresistible offers:
“The Copy Cure is a step-by-step online training course that shows you how to write copy that’s powerful, persuasive, and 100% YOU, so people will love — and buy — what you sell.” — Marie Forleo
“We Help Writers Make Money. Our courses are only open for enrollment a few weeks out of the year. This means we can dedicate more time to our students. (This makes our students happy.) To get notified whenever a course goes live, be sure to sign up below.” — Tim Denning
“This micro-offer strategy made me $16 million. Become a part of the Micro-Hacks Lab today for just $7 per month and get 77 science-backed micro-hacks. Each one of these has the potential to make you $1000 per month.” — Bushra Azhar
Easy to spot when you see them, right?
They’re the ones that make you pause, pay attention, and start thinking “Wait… should I get this?”
But here’s the tricky part — while it’s easy to recognize a great offer, it’s surprisingly hard to create one.
So let’s fix that.
The 3 Rules of an Irresistible Offer
A great offer doesn’t happen by accident.
It’s built the right way and positioned to land with the right people.
To get there, you need to make three simple decisions:
One audience
One problem
One solution
That’s it.
Get those three right, and you can build a highly profitable business — easily in the range of $500K to $1M per year.
Scaling past that? Different ball game. But for now, let’s stay focused.
Next, I’ll break down exactly what those three parts mean — and how to make them work for you.
Target One Audience
I can’t say this loud enough: you need to know exactly who you’re talking to.
If you don’t, how could you possibly know what they want?
And here’s the thing — your audience shapes your message [ https://substack.com/redirect/0cd4a5ae-3057-42d5-a401-7ceed45e01f8?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].
You don’t talk to your boss the same way you talk to your best friend, right? Same rule applies here.
But don’t stop at basic demographics like age or location. Go deeper.
Think psychographics. What do they believe? What do they want? What keeps them up at night?
For example — the pitch for this newsletter is simple:
"For curious passive income hunters and contrarian professionals."
Meaning:
Either you’re building and monetizing side hustles
Or you’re getting paid by a client or employer to do it at scale
That’s one audience (but two life situations). Clear and specific.
Choose One Existing Problem
Now it’s time to get inside their heads.
Find out what’s bugging them. The stuff they Google at midnight.
Here’s how:
Check your most popular posts
Read through comments and DMs — what questions come up again and again?
See what big creators in your niche are talking about
Then, make a quick list of 5 problems or questions you keep seeing.
Done? Good.
Now get even more specific.
If your people want to lose weight, don’t promise to turn them into Olympians overnight.
Nobody buys that.
An irresistible offer tackles one clear problem.
Just one step forward. Could be a small one. Could be a big one. But it should be the thing that keeps them awake at night.
That’s the sweet spot.
Present One Specific Solution
Here’s where a lot of creators slip up.
They either go too big or too small.
Coaching for a tiny problem? Overkill.
A $9 eBook promising to make you rich? C’mon.
When you pick the solution you’re going to offer, keep these two things in mind:
How bad does this problem hurt?
On a scale from 1–10, where does it land?
If it’s a 9 or 10, you can justify a bigger, pricier offer.
If it’s a 3 or 4, a quick fix or micro-offer makes way more sense.
How much can your audience actually afford?
Truth is — most people can stretch for something around $200–$300 if they truly see the value.
Anything under $30 is an easy, impulsive yes.
Higher than that? Still possible, but it takes more trust and a stronger pitch.
So ask yourself:
What’s the specific solution I’m offering?
What result will it give them?
And how will they know it worked?
Yes — it should fix their problem. But go deeper.
What changes when it works?
Do they land their first client?
Lose those last 5 stubborn pounds?
Double their newsletter open rates?
Be crystal clear on the outcome.
Example:
I came across a Fiverr course the other day, and their pitch was super clear:
“If you follow our tactics, you’ll land your first Fiverr order within a week.”
Simple. Tangible. Easy to picture.
And hey — even if what you’re selling isn’t that clear-cut, make it tangible anyway.
Even if you’re writing funny takes on the news, you can still promise something like:
“You’ll laugh more and feel a little better about the weird world we live in.”
Remember: people don’t buy features. They buy results.
So be clear on what result you’re selling.
How To Describe Your Offer So It’s Irresistible
If you made the right moves so far, your offer’s probably already halfway irresistible.
But let’s crank it up.
Take this example:
“I help busy moms lose 5 kg with my self-paced workout program.”
Clear audience: busy moms
Clear solution: self-paced program
Clear result: lose 5 kg
Good.
But we can make it stronger by adding a few things:
Credibility
Answer this: why should people listen to you?
Best case, you’ve got results to share.
If not — borrow them.
You can use:
Research (what studies or experts say)
What’s worked for others
A money-back guarantee (instantly boosts trust with strangers)
Time Reference
People want results fast.
If you can promise a timeline, do it.
“Lose 5 kg in 30 days” hits harder than “Lose 5 kg someday.”
And if you genuinely don’t know how long it’ll take?
Say something like “Faster than you thought possible” — and then be real about the fact that, yes, we all crave instant results.
Urgency
Give people a reason to act now.
Maybe:
The price goes up in a week
A bonus disappears after today
New subscribers won’t get access to past issues unless they pay (like Substack’s archive locks)
People hate missing out. Use it.
Numbers
Numbers sell. Period.
They cut through the fluff and give your offer weight.
Use numbers for:
Results: “5X your creative output”
Deadlines: “in 7 days”
Social proof: “This strategy worked for 10 clients”
Why? Because numbers bring clarity. And clarity converts better than cleverness.
Marie Forleo promises she’ll teach you how to write copy that’s 100% you and still sells.
Clear. Relatable. That’s what you’re aiming for.
Now Let’s Upgrade That Offer
We started with:
“I help busy moms lose 5 kg with my self-paced workout program.”
Decent. It checks the basic boxes.
But now, let’s layer on the good stuff we just talked about:
“I help busy moms lose 5 kg in 10 days using science-backed strategies (the same ones I used). My self-paced workout program includes a full 7-day money-back guarantee.”
See the difference?
It’s clearer, faster, more credible, and carries less risk.
That’s what makes people buy.
Final Words
When you’re starting out, business feels tough.
It’s confusing. Overwhelming. Slow.
But as you stack up the right skills — the kind that actually move the needle — things speed up.
Results show up faster. Money starts coming in. Confidence builds.
And if I had to name the one skill that changes everything?
Selling through your writing [ https://substack.com/redirect/71b59cb3-107b-4113-bee3-ac12466731a4?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].
Nail that, and watch how quickly your life changes.
