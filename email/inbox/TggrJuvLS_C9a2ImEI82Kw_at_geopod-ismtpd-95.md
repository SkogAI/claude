"But, Boots!", I can hear you cry, "Aren't developer jobs being taken by AI? What's even *the point of learning to code?* "

My dear apprentice, if every spell I cast required me to ponder my orb, no magic would ever get done! AI - or LLMs if you're savvy - are wonderful tools for learning and maintaining code. However, in much the same way a power drill didn't replace the need for carpenters, AI can make us more efficient but we still need a human in the loop.

We recently published two separate articles on this very subject for your perusal here ( https://blog.boot.dev/education/vibe-coding-hell/?utm_campaign=website&utm_medium=email&utm_source=sendgrid.com ) and here ( https://blog.boot.dev/computer-science/18-months-with-gpt-4/?utm_campaign=website&utm_medium=email&utm_source=sendgrid.com ). To summarize, in regards to AI in development we believe that:

* Developers will continue to use copilot-like tools
* Developers' Google search queries are going to AI more and more
* There's demand for developers to build new apps that use LLM APIs
* Learning to code while using AI can be really dangerous for your self-improvement - it must be done responsibly
* Developers will continue to develop, and they must know the fundamentals well

The market for knowledgeable and skilled developers continues to grow. Do you want to be a part of it?
