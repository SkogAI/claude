Hi there.
 
Today I have some difficult news to share. We're shutting down Rube.
 
I know that's not what you want to hear. You connected your most important apps, your email, your calendar, your work tools, and
let Rube into your daily workflow. That trust meant everything to us, and we didn't make this decision lightly.
 
I want to explain why.
 
Composio started as an infrastructure for developers building AI agents. Rube started as a way for anyone to automate their apps
with AI. Two different audiences, two different products. But underneath, we were building the same thing: access to 1000+ apps,
auth layer, tool execution engine. Two brands, two codebases, one team trying to do both well.
 
We knew we could build something better if we stopped splitting our effort. So we put everything into Composio.
 
What that means for you:
We built Composio For You to carry forward what Rube did. Same 1000+ apps, but now accessible through MCP and CLI. That means you
can use them inside Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, or any MCP client you already work in. No separate app. Your AI connects to
your apps right where you build.
 
We're also adding multi-account support so you can link all your accounts in one place.
 
Here's what happens next:
New signups and upgrades stopped today. Rube Chat shuts down April 20, so export your chats and recipes before then. We'll process
full refunds for every paying user by May 1. No action needed from your end. Rube MCP stays live until May 15, then all data gets
deleted and API keys get revoked.
 
We're giving every Rube user 3 months of free access to Composio For You to help you make the switch. Just sign up here:
dashboard.composio.dev/login [https://dashboard.composio.dev/login]
 
For detailed answers on refunds, data, API keys, and more, refer to the FAQs: rube.app/deprecation [https://rube.app/deprecation]
 
Questions? Reply to this email. I read them.
 
Thank you for believing in what we were building. We're focused on one thing now: building the best infrastructure for AI agents
to work with the apps you use every day. Rube was part of figuring that out, and every one of you helped shape where we're going
next.
