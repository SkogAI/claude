Guiding less experienced team members can be very time-consuming. Our customers have shared how they solved this with Termius. In this newsletter, we'll provide tips to make your team more productive and save you time in the long run.

🔌 Everyone Can Connect (If You Can)

Many teams complained that members were wasting too much time searching for connection details and credentials scattered in different places. And when they couldn't find the correct ones, they had to track down the right person to ask. Termius simplifies this process so your teammates won't interrupt anyone’s workflow just to find the server name or IP address.

Store your infrastructure in a Team vault for seamless sharing. This ensures all team members can access the same structure of hosts and groups, with up-to-date connection details. This way, if you can connect, anyone can.

For more precise control and security, use multiple shared vaults. This allows you to customize access to different parts of your infrastructure based on team member’s roles and expertise.

🤔 Known Hosts Used The Right Way

Known hosts typically aren't used as intended. This powerful security mechanism is often treated as "Accept Cookies." Instead of verifying a host's fingerprint, we tend to hit 'Yes' without thinking—especially when connecting from a new device or to a server set up by another team member.

With Termius, known hosts are synchronized and shared. Your team will never see the 'add to known host' prompt unless the host has actually been altered. And that’s when you should sound the alarm.

📄 Snippets - Knowledge Base That Works

Taking time off should be relaxing, but outdated documentation can inevitably lead to calls and messages from your teammates.

Instead of error-prone copy-pasting from documentation, share your Snippets with the team. This allows teammates to execute pre-tested, labeled commands you use in your daily work.

For a multi-step scenario - label each snippet with "Step #" and organize them into packages so your team can easily find the solutions when problems arise.

🔑 Set up SSH Keys for the Team

Provisioning team access to remote machines is time-consuming. You have to explain how to generate SSH keys, request public keys from each person, and export them to servers. This back-and-forth communication is frustrating, often making you think, "It would be so much quicker if I could just do it myself!"

To streamline this process, use Termius to generate SSH keys for all team members with just a few clicks. Once the keys are generated, share them with your team via an encrypted vault, ensuring everyone has the necessary access without the usual hassle.

＊ Secure Password Sharing with Identities

When trying to move quickly, team members may share server credentials through unsecured channels like corporate messengers or emails, risking security breaches. To prevent password leaks, save credentials as "Identities" and share them via encrypted vaults.

Create an Identity with a username and password, then move it to a vault your teammates can access. Assign this Identity to specific hosts and groups to enable one-click connections. This approach mitigates the risk of sharing passwords since everyone on the team can connect easily.
