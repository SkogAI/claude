## While Kubernetes clusters might not be 'the basics'...

... keeping your secrets out of plaintext is. Thanks to PM Brian for the reminder.

The External Secrets Operator project now officially supports ngrok (https://d2v8tf04.na1.hubspotlinks.com/Ctc/5F+113/d2v8tf04/VWNxLm2LrkFBW7BD7KH5WqltHW4TqGZG5FdPl4N8SvFG43pyd0W8wLKSR6lZ3kyW8LS0Qs7cdd-BVBlH946z3vGkW8F10fw5CyjxDW460yjd6HQQbnN3YfKX_8ScsVW3JN85Z5wCyTRW2RMX0g299S1QW2V6Wyt8_s4MYW3_yp8g6k6WHqW5QBXCj27-8L6W71zccp5y9CfdN4j9pQ9_wJWBW6vVtYw80ykRGW66GDl44qKDSvW7VYybM2gGb_gW71y1xh1CMg5WW6CPSmM6Bm3NdW1c2d8Y39Q-9nW6wJynV1wYmFMW2MmyJF8RYwpYW23RFkB3-CQ5nN1v0XzKn0SvRW40pHfn5lfq5xW6Pr0Dk2L3DM-W9233lm6R8gWmW5dwpjX1X32sBW4G-3YP4xmkXbW9hkY8H5_kfT3f8lq4n804 ) thanks to Stacks, one of our infra engineers. Pipe the secrets you already store in HashiCorp Vault, AWS Secrets Manager, and others directly into your ngrok traffic policies.

```
apiVersion: external-secrets.io/v1
kind: SecretStore
metadata:
  name: ngrok
spec:
  provider:
    ngrok:
      auth:
        apiKey:
          secretRef:
            name: ngrok-credentials
            key: api-key
      vault:
        name: my-vault # ngrok vault name for storing secrets
```
