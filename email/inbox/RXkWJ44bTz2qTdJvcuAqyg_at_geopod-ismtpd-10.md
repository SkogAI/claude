Erm, what are "The Training Grounds" ( https://www.boot.dev/training?utm_campaign=website&utm_medium=email&utm_source=sendgrid.com ) ?

In case you missed all our messages about it, it's a new tab in Boot.dev (take a look at your top nav bar, it's between "courses" and "pricing/billing"). Once there, you can generate personalized challenges (or search for existing ones) whenever you want! One of the biggest struggles with a platform like Boot.dev is some students want more practice on loops, some want more practice with lists, some with sets... and if we just keep adding practice challenges the courses get SUPER long. Which no one wants.

So, the training grounds is where you should go whenever you feel you're moving too fast through the courses. You'll be able to stop, take a breath, and practice up on the stuff you've already learned. And most importantly, we've tuned the experience to *generate challenges based on where you're at* in the curriculum:

* *Personalization* : You'll practice what you've learned in the courses, and won't be given challenges that require knowledge you haven't learned yet.
* *Spaced repetition* : We use a time-variable algorithm to surface topics that you've learned in the recent and no-so-recent past so that they stick in your long-term memory.
* *Mastery* : We weight toward topics that you struggled with to ensure that you master them before moving on.
* *Correctness* : We have backend systems that run the challenges to ensure the solutions work before they are presented to you.
* *Variety* : There are 3 types of challenges (write the code, fix the bug, interview question) and we select one based on the topic and to keep things fresh for you.
