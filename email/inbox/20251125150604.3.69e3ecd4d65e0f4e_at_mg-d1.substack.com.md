AI isn’t going to take your job. But... those who only use AI for small tasks? They’re the ones who will be replaced. 
They’ll lose out to the professionals who see AI as a core part of their business setup. Their infrastructure.
Want to be the second kind? Here’s how you make the switch.
Consider becoming a free or paid subscriber:
The Workflow Shift
Right now, most professionals are using AI for the small stuff.
Drafting social media posts.
Whipping up email copywriting.
Brainstorming quick ideas.
That’s fine. Keep doing that!
But there’s a much bigger way to use AI. Almost no one is talking about it. And once you grasp it, your whole game changes.
Stop seeing AI as a fast helper for your to-do list.
Instead, start seeing AI as something that can delete entire chunks of work from your week. Forever.
Let me explain what that looks like...
The Infrastructure Mindset
I’v been helping a guy that’s working on something big right now. He’s building a tool for himself. It lets him create a full, sales-ready funnel [ https://substack.com/redirect/7a75e0bf-ddb7-4d63-80f8-020d78e922ab?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] in just a few hours.
This isn’t some quick fix. It’s not about letting an AI website builder “slap together” a page in five minutes.
We’re talking about funnels that are built to work. They:
Use best-in-class marketing frameworks [ https://substack.com/redirect/479ba536-9679-4554-93c0-2ff4137aa4c5?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].
Have all the right tracking set up (like UTMs and backup systems).
Plug right into other marketing tools.
Sound exactly like the brand voice.
Actually convert [ https://substack.com/redirect/2215d978-49aa-49b3-a027-f39aeabcdbbe?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ] because they are smart and strategic.
See the difference? This is way beyond just letting AI do a quick job for you. The goal is to be super fast without losing quality or strategy.
So, stop asking: “What small task can AI help me with today?”
Instead, you should be thinking about this:
What parts of my work do I do over and over again?
Think about everything you touch when you start a new campaign for example. It’s a long list, right?
Setting up the ad account.
Building the landing page.
Getting the email sequence ready.
Making sure the analytics are tracking.
Connecting the CRM.
Setting up the pixel.
Automating the lead flow.
Writing the content.
Getting the SSL certificate.
The graphic design stuff.
Setting up the shopping cart.
Dealing with ad problems.
Fixing email delivery issues.
...and so on.
Now, dream a little.
Ask yourself: What does my perfect setup look like?
Don’t worry about what’s possible. Don’t think about what you can build today. What would you want if you could just snap your fingers?
What tools would you use?
How would all the data talk to each other?
What would be totally automatic?
What would you keep manual for that human touch?
What do you have to touch every single time you launch something new?
The best way to figure this out? Take a week. Keep a quick journal of what you do every day.
Then, when you are actually building a sales funnel or a customer journey, write down every step you or your team takes.
That is the stuff AI can turn into a system for you.
Start Small, Think Big
Important: You don’t have to start where others are at.
The guy I mentioned have been vibe-coding apps for a while now. He have a lot of experience making software. That background is what makes his current project possible for him.
You might not be there yet. And that’s totally fine.
Start smaller, but think bigger.
Just pick one or two things you do all the time in your daily work.
Maybe it’s setting up a landing page with your usual sections and copywriting.
Maybe it’s the email sequences that always follow your winning formula.
Maybe it’s making ad images that match your brand.
Maybe it’s the campaign report that pulls data from a bunch of places.
Build an AI workflow around just that first.
Then, add another process.
Then, another.
Over time, you’ll connect these small workflows. And boom! You’ve built a complete system.
The goal might not be full automation.
Let’s not trying to get AI to do everything without you. That’s a mistake.
There’s a human part that really matters. That’s your expertise. Your smart thinking. Your unique personality.
That stuff is special. It’s what makes your creative output actually work. It’s what will set you apart from everyone else who just lets AI do all the work.
What this guy is building lets him:
Use his proven concepts on a huge scale.
Get rid of the boring, technical setup.
Move fast without losing quality.
Keep the human part where it counts.
AI handles the plumbing, while he handle the strategy.
Here’s a simple example to make this crystal clear.
Think about one full project you do often. Let’s say it’s launching a new lead magnet funnel.
Write down every single step:
Buy the domain.
Set up the domain’s DNS.
Get the SSL certificate.
Add the domain to the landing page tool.
Build the landing page.
Write the landing page copywriting using your best ideas.
Set up tracking pixels.
Create the thank you page.
Write the thank you page copywriting.
Send the free gift.
Set up the email automation.
Write the welcome email series.
Connect it all to the CRM.
Set up the retargeting audience.
Create the ads.
Set up the ad campaign.
Set up conversion tracking.
The Roadmap
Now look at that list and ask:
Which steps are just boring, repetitive setup? (Things like tracking, pixels, and connecting automation.)
Which steps need your smart thinking? (Like your copywriting frameworks, audience ideas, and how you present the offer [ https://substack.com/redirect/f7e84209-ff64-4caa-9dee-ff8f3f5ac42c?j=eyJ1IjoiNHFkNTF0In0.3VeGxV4zk4PWCIHpd0Onx-O6RwY6Z7JeNCNSiNEVLgU ].)
Which steps could AI handle if it knew your rules? (Like writing copy using your winning structures.)
Which steps should you keep manual? (The final check of the copywriting, the big strategic choices… These need your expertise.)
That is your roadmap.
The goal is to build a full customer journey in hours, not days or weeks.
Is that a big goal? Yes.
Will you get it exactly right the first time? Probably not.
But here’s what I know for sure:
Every step you take toward that goal makes you faster, more efficient, and a more powerful professional.
If you can cut the time it takes to build a funnel from 2-3 days down to 4-5 hours? That’s a huge advantage over everyone else. If you can keep the quality high while moving five times faster than your competition, you win.
This is all about your dream setup.
Whatever that looks like for you.
The only real question is: How well do you know your own work?
Because that’s the secret sauce here.
It’s not about knowing how to code. It’s not about being a tech expert.
It’s about deep workflow understanding.
You need to know your process so well that you can teach the AI how to do it.
Stop thinking of AI as a little helper.
Start thinking of AI as your workflow infrastructure.
It all comes down to these four steps:
Pick one work task you do all the time.
Write down every single step.
Figure out what can be turned into a system.
Then start building.
You don’t need to build what others are building.
You need to build what deletes YOUR repetitive work while keeping YOUR strategic edge.
The professionals who figure this out in the next year will have a massive advantage over everyone else still using AI for one-off tasks.
Is this easy if you don’t have tech skills or haven’t built a few apps? No. It’s not.
But it’s a skill you can learn. If you put in the time, you will be light-years ahead of everyone else.
Let me be super clear, though.
Your frameworks and process have to be good. If you have a bad process, no AI on the planet is going to take bad input and turn it into gold.
Ok, that’s it for today.
