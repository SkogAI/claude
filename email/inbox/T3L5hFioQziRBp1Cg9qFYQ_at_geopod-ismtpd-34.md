🔒 Post-Quantum Ready SSH
------------------------

Termius is one of the first SSH clients to support mlkem768x25519-sha256 , the latest quantum-resistant key exchange that protects your SSH connections against threats that don't even exist yet.

Quantum computers, once they arrive, could break today's encryption like it's a simple padlock. However, attackers may already be recording encrypted traffic today, waiting for the day when they can decrypt it.

That's where Termius steps in. With mlkem768x25519-sha256 , your connections are protected by a dual-lock system:

* *ML-KEM* — A NIST-approved post-quantum key encapsulation mechanism, designed to resist quantum attacks.
* *X25519* — A widely used, efficient elliptic-curve key exchange algorithm (pre-quantum).

Together, mlkem768x25519-sha256 provides quantum-safe encryption. Even if quantum computers become a reality tomorrow, sessions using this method would still be secure.

*What You Need to Do* : Just update Termius, and you're protected.

mlkem768x25519-sha256 is supported in *OpenSSH 9.9* and is the default key exchange method in *OpenSSH 10.0*. Termius automatically tries the strongest security available when you connect. No settings to change. Your workflow stays the same, your security gets upgraded.

While quantum-resistant encryption isn't yet mainstream, we know your sensitive data requires long-term protection. By implementing the new algorithm now, Termius ensures that your SSH sessions are secured not just today, but for decades to come.
