Hi Emil Skogsund,
Langfuse is an open source project with a large open source community of self-hosters. Today we extend our commitment to open
source and our community.
 

https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-06-04-open-sourcing-langfuse-product

 
We just released [https://github.com/langfuse/langfuse/releases/tag/v3.65.0] all previously gated product features into our open
source core. This makes the product fully-featured for our self-hosting users - there are no practical changes for users of
Langfuse Cloud.
 


Why are we doing this?

We deeply appreciate our community of multiple tens of thousands of users on Langfuse Cloud, >5M docker pulls, thousands of active
self-hosted deployments that we are aware of and our community on GitHub (we just broke through 12,000 stars
[https://github.com/langfuse/langfuse/] and >1,500 Discussion Threads [https://github.com/orgs/langfuse/discussions]).
 
The LLM landscape changes daily. By making Langfuse more open, we aim to gather more feedback from the community and iterate on
the product faster than ever with all of you.
 
Our community makes Langfuse the top choice for developers and helps keep it relevant as we all figure out how the best AI
applications are built and evaluated.
 
Want to learn more? We just published a blog post [https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-06-04-open-sourcing-langfuse-product] with more
details on this change.
 


How can you help Langfuse move faster? 

The best ways to contribute to Langfuse (in ascending order of difficulty): 
 1. Tell your friends/colleagues about Langfuse / tweet about it / spread the word (this really helps!)

 1. Help by upvoting relevant feature requests [https://github.com/orgs/langfuse/discussions/categories/ideas]

 1. Comment on issues [https://langfuse.com/issues] or contribute PRs
    [https://github.com/langfuse/langfuse/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md]
