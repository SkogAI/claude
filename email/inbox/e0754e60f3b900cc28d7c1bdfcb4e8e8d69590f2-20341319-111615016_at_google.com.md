Hello Google Maps Platform customer,

We’re writing to inform you about updates we are making to the Google Maps  
Platform Terms of Service[1], Service Level Agreement[2], and Acceptable  
Use Policy (AUP)[3], which will be effective starting March 1, 2025.

We’ve provided additional information below to help you understand the  
specific changes to be implemented.

What you need to know

    - Terms of Service[4]: The terms will include specific modifications and  
clarifications to the payment sections for India and Mexico.

    - Service Level Agreement[5]: We added new definitions of a Failed  
request and Monthly Uptime Percentage to align with how developers  
experience service outages. Failed requests are now assessed based on  
server-side errors. The Monthly Uptime Percentage ratio is now assessed by  
evaluating the ratio of failed requests to all requests over the month.

    - Acceptable Use Policy[6]: We added new provisions on dangerous  
activities, harassment and discrimination, privacy violations, limiting  
sensitive consumer opportunities, derived personal information, child  
endangerment, and weapons technology. We are launching a new Help Center[7]  
to provide clear explanations of the Acceptable Use Policy provisions and  
illustrative examples.

What you need to do

No action is required from you. This update will be effective starting  
March 1, 2025.

To learn more about the specific changes, please review the updated  
terms[8]. You can find the previous versions at the bottom of the relevant  
term pages.

We’re here to help

If you have questions or need assistance, please contact Google Maps  
Platform Support[9].
