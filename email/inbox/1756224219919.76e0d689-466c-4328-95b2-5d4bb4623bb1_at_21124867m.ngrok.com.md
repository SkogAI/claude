Block threats, protect upstreams, and get WAF off your to-do list

With a pair of Traffic Policy actions and a few lines of YAML that you can tailor to your traffic, you can now drop malicious requests before they hit your upstreams and halt protected data from leaving your network:

on_http_request:
- actions:
- type: owasp-crs-request
config:
on_error: halt
on_http_response:
- actions:
- type: owasp-crs-response
config:
on_error: halt

Our WAF works without deploying new infra or adding yet another hop between you and your legit customers, and you can always poke around your Traffic Inspector for real-time logs of block requests and their potentially malicious payloads.

Close the door on other bad connections, too

Some requests aren't trying to exploit a specific vulnerability, but just prod where they shouldn't or prod a bit too often. You can send a 403 Forbidden response as a no thanks, but what if the request doesn't even deserve that?

The new close-connection action closes the door before the request even hits your endpoint, much less your upstreams.

on_http_request:
- expression:
- "$SOME_EXPRESSION_TO_FILTER_UNWANTED_TRAFFIC"
actions:
- type: close-connection
