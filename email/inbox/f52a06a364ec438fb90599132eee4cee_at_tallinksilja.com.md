Hejsan.

Du fick ett mail den 20 augusti 2021 gällande bolagets regler gällande pins och ansiktsmasker, den texten kan du bortse ifrån.
Ber om ursäkt att det förekommit olika besked om detta.

Bifogar den text som finns på Insider, det är vad som gäller.



Due to the worsening virus situations in our home markets and regarding the mask wearing regulations in our ships' flag states and considering our customers' expectations, Tallink Grupp's Management Board decided to update the regulations of mask wearing among our on board personnel.

According to the different mask wearing regulations in our ships' flag states and considering the expectations of our customers from different markets and the necessity of amplifying the message of safe travelling among our certain client segments, the Management Board of Tallink Grupp decided that mask wearing is obligatory in the ships' public spaces among our crew members working on the Estonian flag vessels, strongly recommended for the crew members of the Finnish flag vessels and kindly recommended for crew members on board Swedish flag vessels.

In crew areas and rooms, the wearing of a face mask is obligatory only for those who up to day haven't received any doses of the vaccine. After a one dose of a vaccine the crew member is allowed to move and work without a mask in crew areas.

For increasing the sense of safety among our passengers and colleagues, we are going to hand out pins with a message "I'm vaccinated!" among our personnel in the offices, ships, hotels and restaurants, who at least have received one dose of a vaccine. Wearing the pins help to convey the additional safety message and shows to our colleagues and clients that we care about their health and well-being.

We are very thankful for the colleagues who are vaccinated in the sake of our all safety and for keeping the society open. We know that up to day 90% of the crew members of our Estonian flag vessels and 80% of our colleagues working in our hotels are vaccinated, which is more than the average vaccination level among Estonian adult population. We are happy to say that we are role models for others and show that we are doing everything that we can so that the Estonian society stays open and the movement between our countries remains free.


Med vänliga hälsningar / Best regards

BJÖRN ÅDÉN
