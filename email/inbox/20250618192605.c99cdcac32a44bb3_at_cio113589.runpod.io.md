Yesterday, we launched the Runpod Hub ( https://www.runpod.io/console/hub?createListing=false ) — a new, community-based tool that allows users to deploy faster, and creators to share their work without worrying about infrastructure. Here's our announcement ( https://x.com/runpod_io/status/1935401376164823113 ) on X.

It's a game changer, and we are thrilled to support our community of builders. With the Hub, we're breaking down barriers to building great AI — together.

For users, the Hub allows you to host open-source models with two clicks. You can harness of the power of Runpod Serverless without any unnecessary steps for configuration — unless, of course, you want to go deep.

For creators, the Hub empowers you to take to the stage and share your work with tens of thousands of Runpod users. Fork, remix, publish. Help your community thrive.

We've created a video to help you get started building on the Hub. Here's what you'll learn from How To Deploy Serverless Endpoints on Hub ( https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FP0bb1oPOFI ):

******************
What you'll learn:
******************

*
How to select the repo of your choice
* How and where to review the configs
* How to create your endpoint (this is where you deploy!)
* How to manage your endpoint

*******************
How to get started:
*******************

* Choose "Hub" from the left menu
* Select your pre-built repo
* Click "Deploy [version]", on the right
*
Click "Next"
* Click "Create Endpoint"

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sakana AI has introduced the Darwin Gödel Machine (DGM), an AI system that can literally rewrite its own Python code to improve performance. Using evolutionary algorithms, DGM iteratively modifies itself, tests the changes, and keeps the improvements — achieving a 50% success rate on SWE-bench and doubling its performance on multilingual coding tasks from 14.2% to 30.7%.
