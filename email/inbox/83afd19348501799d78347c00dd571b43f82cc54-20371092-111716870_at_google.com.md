Hello Emil,

We’re writing to remind you that as of January 30, 2026, the Dataplex  
Universal Catalog[1] will fully replace the legacy Data Catalog[2] service.  
As a result, the legacy Data Catalog will be discontinued. This will  
require you to upgrade from the legacy Data Catalog to the Dataplex  
Universal Catalog.

We are further simplifying your upgrade process as follows:
    - Simultaneous availability of all resources between Data Catalog and  
Dataplex Universal Catalog
    - IAM roles extension aligning Data Catalog and Dataplex Universal  
Catalog permissions
    - Full Terraform support in Dataplex Universal Catalog
    - Business glossaries are now generally available in Dataplex Universal  
Catalog

We’ve provided additional information below on the steps you must take not  
to lose your cataloging services.

What you need to know

The Dataplex Catalog, now generally available[3], provides a unified  
inventory of your metadata across Google Cloud and on-premises resources,  
with enhanced features and improvements in comparison to the legacy Data  
Catalog capabilities.

All data sources available in the legacy Data Catalog are supported by the  
Dataplex Universal Catalog, along with data search and exploration. New  
tools enable metadata upgrade from Data Catalog to Dataplex Catalog. Once  
upgraded, resources are exclusively managed in Dataplex Catalog and are no  
longer active in Data Catalog.

To facilitate the transition process, by the end of July, 2025, your tags  
and tag templates will be simultaneously available in Dataplex Universal  
Catalog as aspects and aspect types, eliminating the need for manual  
opt-in. Data Catalog IAM roles will be extended with corresponding Dataplex  
Universal Catalog IAM permissions. If you are using Custom IAM roles, you  
will need to grant access to your custom metadata in Dataplex[4] or extend  
your Custom roles with corresponding Dataplex IAM permissions[5].

What you need to do

Before January 30, 2026:

    - Upgrade your Data Catalog users, workloads, and content to Dataplex  
Universal Catalog
    - Follow the steps as described in the Transition from Data Catalog to  
Dataplex Universal Catalog[6] guide

Related changes in Catalog and other Google Cloud services

Effective now:

    - Dataplex Catalog supports certain functionalities differently to Data  
Catalog. To learn more please visit the comparison between Dataplex  
Universal Catalog versus Data Catalog[7] and Metadata management features  
that aren't supported in Dataplex Universal Catalog[8].
    - Data Lineage shows entry details[9] from Dataplex Catalog.
    - InSensitive Data Protection[10], outputs from the discovery action  
Send to Dataplex as tags are made available in Dataplex Catalog as aspects  
under a new aspect type. Learn more about the transition from Data Catalog  
to Dataplex Catalog[11]. This discovery action will stop functioning by  
September 30, 2025. A new action Send to Dataplex Catalog as aspects is  
available now and you must activate it, following the steps described in  
the Add Dataplex Universal Catalog aspects based on insights from data  
profiles[12] documentation, to continue bringing Sensitive Data Protection  
discovery scan outputs to Dataplex Catalog.
    - Business glossaries are now generally available (GA)[13] in Dataplex  
Universal Catalog. With this launch, you can manage glossary[14] taxonomies  
in Dataplex, standardizing business terminologies, and building a shared  
understanding of business context across the enterprise.
If you are a preview user of business glossaries on Data catalog, you can  
now transition to glossaries in GA[15] on Dataplex Universal Catalog.

Effective September 30, 2025:

    - Ingestion of Dataplex entities[16] (including those created as output  
of Dataplex Discovery[17]) will stop functioning. External tables in  
BigQuery published by Dataplex Discovery will be ingested into Dataplex  
Catalog as entries.
    - InSensitive Data Protection[18], outputs from the storage deep  
inspection job action Publish to Dataplex[19] will stop functioning. A new  
action Publish to Dataplex Catalog will become available in Q3, 2025.
