Expanded support for passkeys is here!
The LastPass Passkey Beta has now been expanded to new browser platforms supported by LastPass: Chrome (previously available), Firefox, Edge, Opera, and Safari (coming soon). You can now create and store passkeys for compatible apps and websites on any desktop browser where the LastPass extension is installed.
Get started with passkeys
Open Account Settings in the vault.
Find General Tab > Beta Features > Passkeys: Save and use passkeys.
Click Update on the bottom and enter your master password to verify it’s you.
Close and reopen your browser before adding your first passkey.
Use the LastPass extension in your browser of choice to create, save, and log in with passkeys on sites that support them.
We want your feedback! Please submit feedback directly by clicking “Report a bug” on the Account tab in your LastPass extension menu.  

Note: Make sure you are using the latest version of your LastPass browser extension <https://lnk.lastpass.com/MTI5LU1TUS0wMzgAAAGZceGAMYTNoKPZfwKIP4aGPoCMzQMRYBIRqy9lA1yG0KBb2BYfZp2vqDCt8bDSpQp0M2VvsYw=> to enable the Passkey Beta.
New to passkeys?
Makes sense! Passkeys are an emerging technology that are a simpler and more secure replacement for passwords. Simply put, a passkey is a pair of keys (public and private) that combine and grant you secure access to apps and websites. 

With this expanded beta, LastPass now helps you manage passkeys right alongside the passwords in your vault.

Beta tips and tricks
If you have already turned on the LastPass Passkey Beta in your Chrome extension (previously released), you’re all set. Any passkeys saved to your vault are now available across other browsers where you have LastPass installed.
Passkeys are not a replacement for your LastPass master password. Rather, they will be managed alongside the passwords in your vault so you can securely create, store, and use them with any apps or websites that support passkeys.
You can only use passkeys on apps or websites that have implemented support for the industry-standard FIDO2 passkey authentication flow. Since this technology is evolving, many apps and websites do not support passkeys yet.
