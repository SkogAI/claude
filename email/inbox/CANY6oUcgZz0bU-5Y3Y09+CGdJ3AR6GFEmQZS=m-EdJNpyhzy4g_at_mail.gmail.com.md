Hey folks,

Big news: *Codegen has been acquired by ClickUp!* 🎉

I’m genuinely excited about this. We believe ClickUp is positioned to be
one of the big winners as knowledge work becomes automated, and joining
forces lets us bring what we built at Codegen to a much larger surface area.
What this means for Codegen

The most important operational detail for our users:

   - *We will be sunsetting the standalone Codegen service on January 9th,
   2026.*
   - After that date, Codegen will only be accessible *within ClickUp* (as
   part of the ClickUp product experience).

I know product sunsets can be frustrating. We’re doing this because we
think the best possible version of Codegen’s capabilities lives inside a
horizontally integrated workspace like ClickUp, where agents can operate
with first-party access across docs, tasks, chat, whiteboards, and more.
Why we’re doing this

Over the last year, it’s become clear that the future isn’t “one more tool”
– it’s an integrated system where AI can actually *do work* across the full
context of a company.

We’re excited about ClickUp for the same reason we were excited about
agents in the first place: *when you remove fragmentation, models get
dramatically more useful.* In a converged workspace, an agent can operate
with first-party access across the surfaces where real work happens: docs,
tasks, chat, sheets, whiteboards, and more. That means better context,
fewer handoffs, fewer dead ends, and fewer “sorry, I can’t see that”
moments.

ClickUp has also built real momentum: a large and growing customer base,
rapid adoption of AI features, and a product surface that’s built for
workflows end-to-end. Joining forces lets us take what we built at Codegen
and bring it to a much larger surface area.
The future of code generation

One of the biggest shifts we’ve watched firsthand is that *code agents are
becoming generalist agents*.

Codegen started as “AI that helps you ship code,” but the most valuable
workflows quickly expanded beyond the pull request. The real work includes
context gathering, planning, coordination, and follow-through – which lives
in docs, tasks, and team communication.

This is also why our *ClickUp integration* ended up being one of our most
powerful: it had access to much richer structured context, without some of
the constraints of integrations like Slack (data silos, limited historical
access/search, and context scattered across channels).

Going forward, the best agent experiences will live where the context and
workflows already are – and that’s exactly what a converged workspace
enables.
