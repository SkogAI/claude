Today, we’re releasing multiple new models that deliver breakthrough 
capabilities in retrieval–across accuracy, cost optimization, and supported 
modalities.



This release includes two major additions to the Voyage platform: The Voyage 4 
series <https://mdb.link/voyageai-4-blog>, a new generation of text embedding 
models featuring industry-first shared embedding spaces, and
voyage-multimodal-3.5 <https://mdb.link/voyageai-multimodal-3.5-blog>, which 
expands multimodal retrieval by adding native support for video alongside text 
and images. 



The Voyage 4 series: a new frontier in retrieval accuracy

 * 
Introduces a shared embedding space, allowing cross-model compatibility for 
all embeddings generated within the Voyage 4 series and providing greater 
flexibility in how accuracy, latency, and cost tradeoffs are managed.

 * Enables flexible model selection across the retrieval pipeline, such as 
usingvoyage-4-large for high-fidelity indexing, voyage-4-lite for 
high-throughput queries, andvoyage-4-nano for local development. 
 * voyage-4-large supersedes voyage-3-large as our most accurate model, 
leveraging a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture to deliver frontier-level 
retrieval accuracy while remaining cost-efficient at scale.
Read the Voyage 4 blog  <https://mdb.link/voyageai-4-blog>



Voyage-multimodal-3.5: introducing video retrieval

 * Extends our industry-leading multimodal capabilities to video retrieval, 
enabling semantic search over video content using natural language queries.
 * The first production-grade video embedding model to support Matryoshka 
embeddings, enabling flexible dimensionality (2048, 1024, 512, or 256).
 * Improves standard text retrieval accuracy by 5.22% from voyage-multimodal-3 
and comes within 0.29% ofvoyage-3-large.
