<h3>Build for someone specific</h3>
<p>Werdelin is tired of people asking him why “Catbox” doesn’t exist, and no, it isn’t because he has a bone to pick with felines. Barkbox has always defined itself by “who they serve”—dogs and people who love them—and not “what they did”—sell subscriptions. “[S]o our next big business was <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBIZSBCdWlsdCBBSSBBZ2VudHMgdG8gTGF1bmNoIGEgTWlsbGlvbiBCdXNpbmVzc2VzIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzgyNSwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vd3d3LmxpbmtlZGluLmNvbS9wb3N0cy93ZXJkZWxpbl9iYXJrLWFpci1kb2dzLWZseS1maXJzdC1hY3Rpdml0eS03MTg0MTg2ODMwNjY4MzI0ODY0LWgwYkovIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI1fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">a [first-class] airline for dogs</a></u>,” he says, “not another stuff-in-a-box business;” and indeed, that airline, <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBIZSBCdWlsdCBBSSBBZ2VudHMgdG8gTGF1bmNoIGEgTWlsbGlvbiBCdXNpbmVzc2VzIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzgyNSwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vYWlyLmJhcmsuY28vIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI2fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Bark Air</a></u>, isn’t just “dog-friendly,” it’s built <em>for </em>dogs, with comfortable travel quarters, a personal concierge, and a set of <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBIZSBCdWlsdCBBSSBBZ2VudHMgdG8gTGF1bmNoIGEgTWlsbGlvbiBCdXNpbmVzc2VzIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzgyNSwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vYWlyLmJhcmsuY28vcGFnZXMvZmFxIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI3fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">FAQs</a></u> literally addressed to your dog.  </p>
<p>According to Werdelin, founders must start by asking themselves who they want to serve for the next 10 years, not by chasing the biggest market or the hottest technology. This is reflected in other Prehype-companies like Ro Health, which started from a specific kind of person with specific needs—men dealing with sensitive health issues like hair loss and erectile dysfunction.</p>
<h3>Know your customer better than their bank</h3>
<p>When you invest in truly knowing your customer, you build what Werdelin calls “relationship capita.l” It’s the advantage you get from having a deep understanding of customers’ needs, aspirations, and values. He thinks of relationship capital as having three areas:</p>
<ol>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Depth:</strong> “How much does a customer feel seen by you?” You can measure this in the small, human gestures—how you respond to their social media posts about your company, and how quickly you do it.</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Density:</strong> “How much do they feel that you belong in the community that you serve?” Think of how Patagonia employees are known to be outdoor enthusiasts, or how Peloton’s team includes the same fitness-obsessed people they design for.</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Durability:</strong> “Are you allowed to offer more than the initial thing that you offered?” As Werdelin puts it, “You can imagine what a Nike hotel would look like, but it’s very difficult to compute what a Hilton shoe would be.” He’s alluding to the idea that some brands earn the right to evolve—when customers trust what you stand for, they’ll follow you into new product categories. In the age of AI, he says companies will have an advantage if they “have very clear permission, authenticity, [and] authority to serve their customers in different ways.”</li>
</ol>
<h3>Know yourself as well as you know your customers</h3>
<p>For Dan, building a business with staying power has gone beyond knowing his customer to knowing himself: It took him years to <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBIZSBCdWlsdCBBSSBBZ2VudHMgdG8gTGF1bmNoIGEgTWlsbGlvbiBCdXNpbmVzc2VzIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzgyNSwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZXZlcnkudG8vY2hhaW4tb2YtdGhvdWdodC9hZG1pdHRpbmctd2hhdC1pcy1vYnZpb3VzIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">admit that he wanted to be a writer</a></u>, a desire that once felt at odds with his identity as a founder. Over time, he learned to design his days around both sides of himself: the part that writes and reflects, and the part that loves business and technology. That clarity of identity made Every itself more durable.</p>
<p>Werdelin calls this “customer-founder fit”: the alignment between who you are and who you serve. Without it, you lack the authenticity and authority to build something that your customers would buy.</p>
<p>In his book, Werdelin offers a framework for finding it—the five Ps: powers, passions, possessions, positions, and potentials. Each is a way of asking where your innate strengths lie and how you can build from them. “Every time that I try to build something to make it successful, I end up not building something great,” he says, “and every time I basically go for, ‘I want to build cool shit with people I like,’ then I end up doing something that seems to resonate with people.”</p>
<h3>A generation of agentic startups is taking shape</h3>
<p>Werdelin believes we’re still in the earliest days of figuring out what “agentic businesses”—companies built around AI agents—will actually look like. He compares it to the early 2000s when he was building an internet video startup before YouTube came around. Back then no one knew how to answer simple questions like: Should videos autoplay when you open a link, or require a click? Should the next one start automatically? As he builds Audos, Werdelin is realizing that many of these basic features simply don’t exist yet for generative AI. </p>
<p>But even amid all this uncertainty, one pattern is becoming more and more clear to Werdelin:</p>
<p>Startups are creating a constellation of agents, each solving a different problem for the same customer; echoing Dan’s idea about how our <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0Ijoi8J-OpyBIZSBCdWlsdCBBSSBBZ2VudHMgdG8gTGF1bmNoIGEgTWlsbGlvbiBCdXNpbmVzc2VzIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzgyNSwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZXZlcnkudG8vY2hhaW4tb2YtdGhvdWdodC90aGUta25vd2xlZGdlLWVjb25vbXktaXMtb3Zlci13ZWxjb21lLXRvLXRoZS1hbGxvY2F0aW9uLWVjb25vbXkiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6Mjl9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">role will shift to being managers of AI agents</a></u>.</p>
