<h2><strong>The eight planning strategies</strong></h2>
<p>When youвЂ™re planning with AI, youвЂ™re running parallel research operationsвЂ”each one a specialized agent gathering different kinds of knowledge. Then you work together: The agents bring findings, you make decisions, and together you combine and distill everything into one coherent plan.</p>
<p>ItвЂ™s much faster for five agents to research in parallel than for a human to plan step by step. Your contribution to the process is taste, judgment, and context about what matters for your product and users. </p>
<p>I use eight research strategies, depending on the fidelity level, which refers to the degree of difficulty. Fidelity One is quick fixes like one-line changes, obvious bugs, and copy updates. Fidelity Two covers features spanning multiple files with clear scope but non-obvious implementation. Fidelity Three covers major features where you donвЂ™t even know what youвЂ™re building yet.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 1: Reproduce and document</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> Attempts to reproduce bugs or issues before planning fixes</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> Fidelity One and Two, especially bug fixes</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Create a step-by-step reproduction guide</p>
<p><strong>Prompt:</strong> вЂњReproduce this bug, donвЂ™t fix it, just gather all the logs and info you need.вЂќ</p>
<p>Right after the launch of <a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vY29yYS5jb21wdXRlciIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoxNX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Cora</a>вЂ™s <a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vY29yYS5jb21wdXRlci9kZWNsYXJlLWVtYWlsLWJhbmtydXB0Y3k_dXRtX3NvdXJjZT1ldmVyeV9iYW5uZXIiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MTZ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">email bankruptcy feature</a>, 19 users were stuck. TheyвЂ™d clicked вЂњarchive everything,вЂќ but the job failed. Instead of guessing the reason for the problem, I told Claude Code: вЂњLoop through the AppSignal logs and diagnose this.вЂќ (AppSignal logs are our error tracking system that records what goes wrong in production.) </p>
<p>Five minutes later, I had a reply: Rate limit errors were being swallowed in production. The job hit GmailвЂ™s limit, failed silently, and never resumed. Users would click вЂњarchive everything,вЂќ see a loading spinner, and wait foreverвЂ”because when one batch failed, the entire job stopped, but we never told the user. That reproduction showed we needed batch processing and job resumption, not just retries.</p>

<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> All fidelities, especially unfamiliar patterns</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Find and summarize relevant blog posts, documentation, and solutions</p>
<p><strong>Agent:</strong> вЂњ<u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvYmVzdC1wcmFjdGljZXMtcmVzZWFyY2hlci5tZCIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoxOX0=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">@agent-best-practices-researcher</a></u>вЂќ</p>

That research took three minutes and prevented hours of trial-and-error debugging. </p>
<p>IвЂ™ve also used this for non-technical decisions: вЂњSaaS pricing tiers best practicesвЂќ returned frameworks for structuring pricing plans. вЂњEmail drip campaign conversion copyвЂќ found proven email templates. вЂњBackground job retry strategiesвЂќ surfaced patterns in how other companies solved that problem at scale.</p>

Next time a similar question comes up, the agent checks these documents first before searching the web. My knowledge base is constantly growing and improving.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 3: Ground in your codebase</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvcmVwby1yZXNlYXJjaC1hbmFseXN0Lm1kIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjIyfQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Finds similar existing patterns</a></u> in your code</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> Anything that might duplicate existing functionality</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Search through your existing code for related implementations</p>
<p>Before adding event trackingвЂ”the system that tracks what users click and whenвЂ”to a new feature, I had an agent search our codebase: вЂњHow do we currently handle event tracking? WhatвЂ™s our pattern for analytics calls? Where do we send events?вЂќ </p>
<p>It found we already had a tracking system that IвЂ™d forgotten about, complete with helper methods, which are reusable bits of code that handles repetitive tasks (in this case, adding tracking). If AI doesnвЂ™t ground itself in your codebase, it often thinks it needs to create a solution from scratch. In this case, instead of reinventing event tracking, we extended the existing pattern. The search prevented building a second, incompatible tracking system and saved time.</p>
<p><strong>How to make this compound: </strong>I created an вЂњ@event-tracking-expertвЂќ agent that distills everything about how we do trackingвЂ”our helper methods, our event format, when to track versus when not to. Now when itвЂ™s planning any feature that needs tracking, that specialist agent runs automatically. I donвЂ™t search the codebase from scratch anymoreвЂ”the expert already knows our patterns.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 4: Ground in your libraries</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvZnJhbWV3b3JrLWRvY3MtcmVzZWFyY2hlci5tZCIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjoyM30=" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Reads source code</a></u> of installed packages and gems</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> When using fast-moving or poorly documented libraries</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Analyze the source code to understand whatвЂ™s possible</p>
<p>I use a Ruby gem called RubyLLM for AI API calls. It updates constantly with new models, new parameters, and new capabilities, but documentation lags behind. So when I need to use it, I have an agent read the gemвЂ™s source code: вЂњLook through the RubyLLM source. What model options are available? What parameters can I pass? Are there any undocumented features in the latest version?вЂќ</p>
<p>The agent comes back with: вЂњVersion 1.9 added streaming support but itвЂ™s not in the docs yet. HereвЂ™s the parameter name and example usage from the test suite.вЂќ</p>
<p><strong>Why this compounds:</strong> Every time you update a dependency (a library your code relies on), the knowledge auto-updates. YouвЂ™re never working with stale information.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 5: Study git history</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvZ2l0LWhpc3RvcnktYW5hbHl6ZXIubWQiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjR9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Analyzes commit history</a></u> (the log of all past changes to your code) to understand intent</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> Refactors, continuing work, understanding вЂњwhyвЂќ</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Research past decisions and their context</p>
<p>I was working on a feature and noticed we were using an outdated version of our EmailClassifier feature, which identifies what a given email is and whether it should stay in the inbox or get briefed. My first thought was: вЂњWhy havenвЂ™t we upgraded this? Let me update it.вЂќ </p>
<p>Before making the change, I had an agent search the git history: вЂњWhy are we using v1? Has anyone tried upgrading to v2?вЂќ </p>
<p>It found a pull request from three months agoвЂ”from a different team memberвЂ”that had upgraded to version two, discovered that version two put inbox emails in the archive and archive emails in the inbox (the opposite of what we want), and deliberately rolled back with detailed reasoning in the PR discussion. Version two changed how it handled edge cases, which would have broken our email scheduling. </p>
<p>That five-minute git search saved me from reintroducing a bug someone else had already debugged and fixed.</p>
<p><strong>Why this compounds:</strong> Institutional memory gets preserved and searchable. New team members inherit the reasoning behind past decisions.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 6: Vibe prototype for clarity</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> Rapid prototyping in a separate environment to clarify requirements</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> Fidelity Three, UX uncertainty, exploratory work</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Quickly build throwaway versions you can interact with</p>
<p><strong>Prompt:</strong> вЂњCreate a working prototype, in the style of a mockup using React and Next, grayscale of XYZвЂќ</p>
<p>For a redesigned email Brief interface, I didnвЂ™t know what layout would feel right. So I vibe coded five different prototypes in Claude, each of which took five minutes to build. I clicked through them, noticed what annoyed me, and showed the best one to a few users.</p>
<p>One user said, вЂњThis layout feels overwhelming and I donвЂ™t know how to archive emails.вЂќ That insight became a requirement in the real planning: вЂњArchive button must be in top-left cornerвЂ”user muscle memory expects it there from Gmail.вЂќ</p>
<p>The prototypes got deleted. The knowledge went into the plan.</p>
<p><strong>Why this compounds:</strong> Vibe coding turns uncertainty into concrete specifications. YouвЂ™re not guessing what users wantвЂ”youвЂ™re showing them options and documenting their reactions.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 7: Synthesize with options</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> Combines all research into one plan showing multiple approaches with tradeoffs</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> End of the research phase, before implementation</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Present 2-3 solution paths with honest pros and cons</p>
<p>After running strategies 1-6, I have an agent synthesize everything: вЂњBased on all this research, show me three ways to solve this problem. For each approach, tell me: implementation complexity, performance impact, maintenance burden, and which existing patterns it matches.вЂќ</p>
<p>For syncing usersвЂ™ Gmail inboxes with Cora so we could display their emails, the synthesis came back with: </p>
<p><strong>Option AвЂ”</strong>Use existing sync system: Fast to implement, but creates overlap with current code and muddies separation of concerns </p>
<p><strong>Option BвЂ”</strong>Real-time sync: Clean architecture, but slow and potential reliability issues<strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong>Option CвЂ”</strong>Build mirror caching system: Best long-term solution, cleanest separation, but most upfront work</p>
<p>In other words, it was suggesting I either: bolt Gmail syncing onto our current system (quick but messyвЂ”like duct-taping a second mailbox to your existing one), fetch emails from Gmail every single time a user opens Cora (clean but slowвЂ”like calling the post office to check your mail instead of having a mailbox), or build our own local copy of the userвЂ™s Gmail that stays in sync (more work upfront, but fastest and most reliable long-termвЂ”like having your own mailbox that updates automatically).</p>
<p>Once I saw that comparison laid out, I was able to make an informed choice in 30 seconds. The agent did the research; I contributed the judgment.</p>
<p><strong>Why this compounds:</strong> Your choice reveals preferences. When I picked Option C and noted, вЂњI prefer widely supported over cutting-edge,вЂќ that preference gets codified. Next time thereвЂ™s a similar decision, the system knows to weight compatibility highly.</p>
<h3><strong>Strategy 8: Review with style agents</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What it does:</strong> Runs the completed plan through specialized reviewers that check for your preferences</p>
<p><strong>When to use it:</strong> Final planning step, before implementation</p>
<p><strong>The agentвЂ™s job:</strong> Catch misalignments with your coding style and architecture preferences</p>
<p>I have three review agents that run automatically:</p>
<p><strong>Simplification agent:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvY29kZS1zaW1wbGljaXR5LXJldmlld2VyLm1kIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI1fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Flags over-engineering</a></u>. вЂњDo we really need three database tables for this? Could one table with a type field work?вЂќ</p>
<p><strong>Security agent:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMvc2VjdXJpdHktc2VudGluZWwubWQiLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6MjZ9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Checks for common vulnerabilities</a></u>. вЂњThis plan allows user input directly into a database queryвЂ”add input sanitization.вЂќ</p>
<p><strong>Kieran-style agent:</strong> <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS9ibG9iL21haW4vcGx1Z2lucy9jb21wb3VuZGluZy1lbmdpbmVlcmluZy9hZ2VudHMva2llcmFuLXJhaWxzLXJldmlld2VyLm1kIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI3fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Enforces my personal preferences</a></u>. вЂњThis uses complex joins (combining data from multiple database tables in a single query). Kieran prefers simple queries. Consider denormalizing (storing redundant data for simpler queries).вЂќ</p>
<p>Plans get better before any code is written.</p>
<p><strong>Why this compounds:</strong> These agents accumulate your taste over time. Every time I indicate, вЂњI donвЂ™t like thisвЂќ or вЂњGood catch,вЂќ the system gets smarter.</p>
<h2><strong>Getting started: Try this today</strong></h2>
<p>You donвЂ™t need to build everything from scratch. IвЂ™ve open-sourced my planning system on <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHA6Ly9naXRodWIuY29tL0V2ZXJ5SW5jL2V2ZXJ5LW1hcmtldHBsYWNlIiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjI4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">EveryвЂ™s Github marketplace</a></u>. Install it in Claude Code, and youвЂ™ll have working /plan slash command and research agents immediately. You can also <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiVGVhY2ggWW91ciBBSSB0byBUaGluayBMaWtlIGEgU2VuaW9yIEVuZ2luZWVyIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6MzkwMywicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vZ2l0aHViLmNvbS9FdmVyeUluYy9ldmVyeS1tYXJrZXRwbGFjZS90cmVlL21haW4iLCJwb3NpdGlvbiI6Mjl9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">use my plugin</a></u> in Claude Code or Droid. </p>
<p>But you can also start more simply by applying the thinking to your next feature: </p>
<p><strong>Pick one Fidelity Two feature youвЂ™re building this week.</strong> It should span multiple files and have clear scope, like adding a new view, implementing a feedback system, or refactoring a component (a reusable piece of your application).</p>
<p>Before prompting Claude Code or Cursor to build it, spend 15-20 minutes researching:</p>
<ol>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Best practices:</strong> How have others solved similar problems? Search the web for blog posts, Stack Overflow discussions, and documentation.</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Your patterns:</strong> How have <em>you</em> solved similar problems? Search your existing codebase for comparable features.</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Library capabilities:</strong> What do your tools actually support? If youвЂ™re using a specific code library, have AI read its documentation or source code</li>
</ol>
<p>Have AI synthesize this research into a plan showing:</p>
<ol>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span>The problem being solved (one clear sentence)</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span>Two or three solution approaches (with honest pros and cons of each)</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span>Which existing code patterns this should match</li>
<li data-list="bullet">
<span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span>Any edge cases or security considerations</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Review the plan and notice your reactions.</strong> When you think вЂњthis is too complexвЂќ or вЂњwe already have a better way to do this,вЂќ donвЂ™t just fix this planвЂ”capture <em>why</em> you think that. Write it down.</p>
<p><strong>Ship the feature based on the plan,</strong> then compare the final implementation to the original plan. Where did you diverge? Why? What would have made the plan better?</p>
<p><strong>Take 10 minutes to codify one learning.</strong> The simplest way: Add it to your CLAUDE.md file. Write one rule: вЂњWhen doing X type of work, remember to check Y,вЂќ or вЂњI prefer approach A over approach B because of reason C.вЂќ</p>
<p>As you accumulate more learnings, create specialized research agents or commands you can call, such as an вЂњEvent Tracking ExpertвЂќ that knows your patterns, or a вЂњSecurity CheckerвЂќ that flags common mistakes. Each agent is just codified knowledge that runs automatically.</p>
<p>That was just one feature, one planning session, one captured learning. Next week, do it again. Reference your notes. See if the second plan is better than the first. In a few months, youвЂ™ll have a system that knows how you think.</p>
