<h2>Knowledge base</h2>
<p><strong><u>“OpenClaw: Our Comprehensive Guide for Beginners”</u></strong> <em>by Dan Shipper and Willie Williams</em>: A Claw is a personal assistant that lives in your messaging apps, rewrites its own code to learn new skills, and acts without being asked. <strong><u>Dan Shipper</u></strong> and head of platform <strong><u>Willie Williams</u></strong> distill everything they’ve learned from running Claws daily: the right mental model (delegate, don’t search), how to stay secure, and how personality emerges from everyday use. Read this before your first conversation with your Claw.</p>
<p><strong><u>“OpenClaw: Setting Up Your First Personal AI Agent”</u></strong> <em>by Katie Parrott/Source Code</em>: Personal AI agents that text you back, order groceries, and write code overnight are no longer stunts—they’re weekly workflows for a growing community of OpenClaw users. At Every’s first OpenClaw Camp, COO <strong><u>Brandon Gell</u></strong>, head of growth <strong><u>Austin Tedesco</u></strong>, <strong>Nat Eliason</strong>, and <strong>Claire Vo</strong> walked 500 subscribers through four distinct setups, from a family assistant in iMessage to a crypto-trading agent with its own bank account. Read this for key lessons on where to start.</p>
<p> <strong><u>“How Claws Took Over Every”</u></strong> <em>by Every Staff/Context Window</em>: Every’s Claws have colonized the company’s Slack, and the patterns that have emerged — agents advising each other, one Claw broadcasting to many humans — hint at what AI-native organizations actually look like in practice. This week’s newsletter maps those interaction types and includes a new <em>AI &amp; I</em> episode where Dan talks to <strong>Sam Gerstenzang</strong> and <strong>Dan Friedman</strong> of Boulton and Watt, a startup incubator focused on unglamorous businesses like medical spas and funeral homes, about building AI-durable companies that Silicon Valley ignores. 🎧 🖥 Watch on <u>X</u> or <u>YouTube</u>, or listen on <u>Spotify</u> or <u>Apple Podcasts</u>. </p>
