Hi Emil Skogsund,
It's Day 4 of Launch Week! Today, we’re taking Experiments in Langfuse to the next level. You can now systematically review
dataset runs faster by annotating directly in the compare view, setting a baseline to view score differences, and using filters to
find outliers.
Watch Marlies demo [https://youtu.be/ltiXILTpoVg] the new features:

https://youtu.be/ltiXILTpoVg

 


Annotations in Experiments Comparison View

Streamline your human review workflow. You can now assign scores and leave comments directly side-by-side with your experiment
results, without losing context or switching tabs, making it easier to do pairwise evaluations.
→ Read more [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-10-23-annotate-from-compare-view]

https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-10-23-annotate-from-compare-view

 


Baseline Comparison

Spot outliers quickly when testing new prompts or models. Select one of your best runs as a "baseline" to instantly see how
metrics for new runs differ from your gold standard.
→ Read more [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-11-06-compare-view-baseline-support]

https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-11-06-compare-view-baseline-support


 


Filters in Compare View

Drill down into your data faster. You can now filter the Compare View to show only specific experiment items. For instance, only
showing item where your evals dropped below a threshold allowing you to focus on the subset that matters.
→ Read more [https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-11-07-compare-view-filters]

https://langfuse.com/changelog/2025-11-07-compare-view-filters

 


Experiment Runner SDK

We've added a new high-level SDK abstraction that makes running experiments much simpler. It includes automatic tracing,
concurrent execution, and flexible evaluation out of the box.
→ Learn more in the docs [https://langfuse.com/docs/evaluation/experiments/experiments-via-sdk#experiment-runner-sdk]

https://langfuse.com/docs/evaluation/experiments/experiments-via-sdk#experiment-runner-sdk


 


Blog: Running and Interpreting Experiments

To get you started with Experiments in Langfuse, we published a guide describing the process to systematically evaluate and
iterate on the quality. We will explain how to run experiments and interpret their results.
→ Learn more [https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-11-06-experiment-interpretation]

https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-11-06-experiment-interpretation

 


Blog: Langfuse in CI/CD Pipelines

As you want to test every new deployment of your LLM application, we also published a guide on running Dataset Experiments as part
of a CI/CD workflow.
→ To Guide [https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-10-21-testing-llm-applications]

https://langfuse.com/blog/2025-10-21-testing-llm-applications
