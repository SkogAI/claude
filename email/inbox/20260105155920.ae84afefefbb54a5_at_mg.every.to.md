<p>The companyвЂ™s pitfall was simple: It gave customers $9.95 a month to see up to 30 movies while the company paid theaters $10 per ticket. As it turns out, you canвЂ™t offer a 96.68 percent discount for very long.</p>
<p>Companies building products powered by AI today face the same trap. In traditional software as a service, once you have paid your developers and Amazon Web Services for storage, it doesnвЂ™t cost you any more if more people use your product. But AI companies pay LLM providers per use. Growth is expensive.</p>
<p>AI vibe coding platform Replit learned this the hard way. The people who use it to code apps and websites pay a monthly fee, plus a bit more if they use it a lot. Meanwhile, Replit has to pay to use the LLMs that power it. Gross marginsвЂ”revenue minus direct costsвЂ”collapsed from 36 percent gross margin last February to <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IEFJIE1hZGUgUHJpY2luZyBIYXJkIEFnYWluIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6Mzg4MiwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vd3d3LnRoZWluZm9ybWF0aW9uLmNvbS9hcnRpY2xlcy9yZXBsaXRzLW1hcmdpbnMtaWxsdXN0cmF0ZS1oaWdoLWNvc3RzLWNvZGluZy1hZ2VudHM_cmM9NjV3ZHp3IiwicG9zaXRpb24iOjd9" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">negative 14 percent</a></u> in April; it was losing money the more customers used it. By July, it had recovered to 23 percent, but itвЂ™s far below the 70 to 85 percent gross margins that traditional SaaS companies enjoyed.</p>
<p>As the co-founder of Y Combinator-backed open-source billing company <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiSG93IEFJIE1hZGUgUHJpY2luZyBIYXJkIEFnYWluIiwicG9zdF9pZCI6Mzg4MiwicG9zdF90eXBlIjoicG9zdCIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vd3d3LmdldGxhZ28uY29tLyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjo4fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Lago</a></u>, which helps companies charge customers and create invoices, IвЂ™ve watched dozens of AI companies hit the same wall. ItвЂ™s clear to me that the era of a one-size-fits-all pricing strategy is over. There is not concrete clear information for founders and teams building with AI and worried about how much to charge when much of their revenue is going toward LLMs.</p>
<p>AI has shrunk not only gross profit margins, but also the margin for error when competition is so fierce. That makes figuring out product pricing and maintaining reasonably healthy margins a full-company effort. If you are building an AI company, more of your team beyond the finance department will need to know about pricing, monetization, and margins.</p>
<p>Founders and builders need to understand the five pricing models that can be adapted to AI tools so companies donвЂ™t face bankruptcy from LLM costs. If you follow these new pricing principles for the AI age, youвЂ™ll understand why your engineering team needs to care about margins as much as your CFO does, and how to structure pricing that turns AIвЂ™s marginal costs from a liability into a competitive advantage.  </p>
<h2><strong>AI pricing models: Where they succeed and where they fail</strong></h2>
<p>LetвЂ™s break down the pricing models on which you can build a sustainable business when much of your costs come from paying LLMs. </p>
<h3><strong>Usage-based pricing: Pay for what you consume </strong></h3>
<p>Usage-based pricing is where you only pay for what you consume. This is how infrastructure providers like Amazon Web Services or OpenAI charge their customersвЂ”per gigabyte stored or token processed. Replit pays OpenAI per token processed, both input and output, instead of with a fixed subscription.</p>
