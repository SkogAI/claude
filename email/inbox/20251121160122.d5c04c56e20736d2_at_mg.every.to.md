<p>When the first trailer for legendary Mexican director <strong>Guillermo del Toro</strong>вҖҳs <em>Frankenstein</em> dropped, the taglineвҖ”four words slammed across the screen in huge white lettersвҖ”sent me scrambling for a Google doc: ONLY MONSTERS PLAY GOD. </p>
<p>As a writer who works in technology and also happens to have an extremely marketable degree in English literature, my immediate impulse was to reach for the AI take: Frankenstein, creation, hubris, founders, modelsвҖ”the <u><a href="https://every.to/emails/click/f91b1e00930c1c7802951fb76da18da9d1d86023a9ec7b653e47ca1b5d584dbf/eyJzdWJqZWN0IjoiRnJhbmtlbnN0ZWluIElzIE5vdCBZb3VyIEFJIE1ldGFwaG9yIChBdCBMZWFzdCwgTm90IExpa2UgVGhhdCkiLCJwb3N0X2lkIjozODQ0LCJwb3N0X3R5cGUiOiJwb3N0IiwidXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9rYXRpZXBhcnJvdHQuc3Vic3RhY2suY29tLz91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249cHJvZmlsZV9jaGlwcyIsInBvc2l0aW9uIjo2fQ==" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Substack essay</a></u> basically wrote itself. </p>
<p>Then I watched the movie, twice. <em>Frankenstein</em> absolutely has something to say about AI. Just not the thing I thought.</p>
<p>HereвҖҷs a quick recap for those of you who havenвҖҷt read the book or have, but itвҖҷs been a while since high-school English: <strong>Mary Shelley</strong>вҖҷs <em>Frankenstein </em>tells the story of an obsessive young academic, Victor Frankenstein (<strong>Oscar Isaac</strong> in the film) who, on a mission to conquer death and push human knowledge to its outermost limits, brings the creature (thatвҖҷs a spectacular <strong>Jacob Elordi</strong> in the movie) to life. Fallout ensues. </p>
<p>Del ToroвҖҷs <em>Frankenstein </em>is a largely faithful adaptation of the 1818 novel, with a number of what IвҖҷd say are acceptable-to-excellent tweaks. But the more I think about it, the more I have an issue with its slogan.</p>
<p>вҖңOnly monsters play GodвҖқ is a catchy tagline aimed at driving views. But thereвҖҷs so much more to this book <em>and </em>this film than Disney-grade platitudes about вҖңreal monsterhoodвҖқ can convey. Because the most interesting part is not the вҖңitвҖҷs aliiiiiveвҖқ moment in the lab (as much as that scene in the movie rips); itвҖҷs what happens after the eyes open, when the question becomes what you owe to what youвҖҷve made, and what kind of monster you become when you walk away.</p>  <div class="ea-block normal">
